# Policy RACI — Strategy, DRS, EPR, EU Acquis

> **Two time horizons.** This matrix now spans **pre-new-law (current state, 2024-2026)** and **post-new-law (2027+)** activities. The new Law on Integrated Waste Management is on track for Government approval Feb-Mar 2026, Assembly approval Aug-Sep 2026, and secondary legislation Feb-Mar 2027 (per Mazreku/MMPHI deck, PR #13). Rows that describe post-new-law activities are tagged **(new-law)** inline. Two new institutional actors — Hybrid Regulator (HReg) and Five Waste Management Zones (WMZ) — are introduced by the new law and only become operational in the post-2027 horizon.

> Layer 2 of 3. Where reforms are queued. ~75% confident — most of these activities exist on paper or in the INDEP/KAS recommendations; few are operationally tested. Cells marked ? mostly track verification issues.

See [`README.md`](README.md) for marker conventions and full actor codes.

## Actors in this matrix

MMPHI · **HReg** · AMMK · MINT · MoFin · MoAg · KOM · **WMZ** · PAS/operators · IND/CON · EU · DON · PRO · ASMB (Assembly, new-law approval phase only)

**New actor codes (introduced by new Law on Integrated Waste Management):**

- **HReg — Hybrid Regulator.** New independent regulator created by the new law. Responsibilities: tariff setting under Full Net Cost Coverage (FNCC) principle, performance monitoring across the waste sector, dispute resolution between municipalities, operators, and producers. Does not yet exist; expected operational from 2027 once secondary legislation passes. Until then, cells in HReg column read "—" (does not exist) for current-state rows.
- **WMZ — Five Waste Management Zones.** Inter-municipal cooperation legally enshrined in the new law: Kosovo's municipalities will be grouped into five waste management zones for shared infrastructure (landfills, transfer stations, sorting facilities) and joint planning. Zones are a structural actor distinct from individual municipalities (KOM column) — zones operate the shared backbone; KOM operate within and participate. Operational from 2027.

## Row notes

**Row 1 — Strategy.** The 2024-2030 strategy was adopted by MMPHI in April 2024 (revising the original 2021-2030 strategy). MMPHI is the clean A,R. AMMK supplies data; MINT, MoFin, MoAg consulted; municipalities and operators are I (informed of national targets but not part of drafting).

**Row 2 — Budget.** Action Plan 2024-2026 = €41M; total CE program 2024-2030 = €77M. Allocation runs through MoFin; the EU's Reform & Growth Facility (€6B 2024-2027) is the largest Consulted source for the Western Balkans bucket. Donors are also C (GIZ, JICA, World Bank).

**Row 3 — DRS design.** Secondary legislation passed 2024. MMPHI is A,R for the design. PROs (Producer Responsibility Organizations) are listed as the eventual R for operation, but no PROs are publicly known to be operational yet — the ? on row 4 captures this.

**Row 4 — DRS operations.** Planned launch January 2025. Verification issue #4 open. The "R ?" on PRO is the document's plan; reality may be no operator yet, or MMPHI directly operating.

**Rows 5-6 — EPR systems.** EPR is the conceptual frame for packaging + WEEE/batteries. MMPHI owns the policy A; PROs are the planned R but don't yet exist as known operators. Industry (producers) are Consulted because EPR shifts cost onto them.

**Row 7 — Source separation mandate.** MMPHI sets the rule; municipalities operate; collection operators (Pastrimi etc.) actually do the separated collection. Multiple R is correct here — separation requires action at all three levels.

**Row 8 — CDW recovery.** Kosovo policy says 80% target. Construction sector is the R (must comply at site). Implementation is reportedly limited — the dossier flags CDW as the largest single illegal-dumping driver.

**Row 9 — Landfill tax.** Recommendation 10 from INDEP/KAS. Doesn't exist yet in Kosovo. If implemented, MoFin would be A,R via legislative amendment.

**Row 10 — Green Public Procurement.** Procurement Regulatory Commission (under MoFin) is A,R. Recommendation 12 from INDEP/KAS. Currently weak.

**Row 11 — EU acquis alignment.** Two A's because there are two distinct accountabilities: MMPHI for *implementing* the acquis, EU for *assessing* compliance. Acceptable double-A here.

**Row 14 — Inter-ministerial CE unit.** Recommendation 2 from INDEP/KAS. Doesn't exist yet. MMPHI as proposed A.

**Row 15 — National material-flow data system.** Recommendation 1 from INDEP/KAS. AMMK is the operational R (it already runs environmental databases); MMPHI accountable for the policy; data quality is widely flagged as fragmented. Note: the new-law NWIS (row 19) is the post-2027 successor / formalization of this system, with mandatory reporting from KOM, WMZ, operators, and PROs.

**Row 16 — (new-law) Drafting the Law on Integrated Waste Management.** MMPHI is A and R for drafting, per the Mazreku/MMPHI deck. EU is C on acquis-alignment review. Government approval (CG = Council of Gov, R for the approval-phase decision) targeted for Feb-Mar 2026. Stakeholders consulted across drafting cycle. HReg does not yet exist so does not participate in its own creation.

**Row 17 — (new-law) Approval phase.** The Assembly (ASMB) is the formal R for adoption (Aug-Sep 2026 target window). MMPHI / Government is A as sponsor. All other actors are I — they are informed of the legislative outcome but do not participate in the vote.

**Row 18 — (new-law) Tariff setting under FNCC.** Full Net Cost Coverage (FNCC) is the new principle that waste tariffs must recover the full net system cost (not subsidized below cost). Hybrid Regulator (HReg) is A,R for setting tariffs under this principle. MMPHI consulted on policy framework, MoFin consulted on fiscal interaction. KOM and WMZ are I (must apply tariffs); operators and PROs are I (must collect them).

**Row 19 — (new-law) National Waste Information System (NWIS).** MMPHI accountable; AMMK operational R (continues its data role from row 15). New under the law: mandatory reporting from KOM, WMZ, operators, and PROs — these are all R because the regime depends on each of them feeding clean data in. HReg is C (uses NWIS data for tariff and performance functions). This is the formalization + expansion of row 15.

**Row 20 — (new-law) Five Waste Management Zones.** MMPHI A,R for establishing the zones (legal designation, perimeter, governance template). Municipalities are R for participating (joining the zone they are assigned to). WMZ as the new structural actor is R for operating the zonal infrastructure once stood up. HReg is C (will regulate zonal tariffs and performance under FNCC).

**Row 21 — (new-law) Dispute resolution post-2027.** HReg is A,R for resolving waste-service disputes between municipalities, operators, producers, and PROs once secondary legislation activates. This replaces ad-hoc ministerial / court-based dispute paths for in-sector disputes. Courts remain a backup avenue (not modeled as a column). MMPHI is C on policy precedent.

## What this matrix shows

- **MMPHI is A on most rows.** It's the load-bearing institution for policy. Its capacity is therefore the binding constraint — and the new law adds drafting, NWIS operation, and zone establishment to its plate.
- **HReg concentrates post-2027 power.** Three of the new-law rows (tariffs, dispute resolution, plus a C role on NWIS and zones) flow through a single not-yet-existing regulator. Standing-up risk is high; HReg capacity will be the new binding constraint after 2027.
- **WMZ is paper architecture.** Five zones legally enshrined but no operational entities yet. Closest analog to PRO column — written into law, not yet instantiated.
- **PRO column is mostly ? or "to-form".** Producer Responsibility Organizations are written into the law but not operationally instantiated. Major implementation gap, now compounded by mandatory NWIS reporting expectations.
- **MoFin appears in 5 rows** (budget, landfill tax, GPP, CE coordination, FNCC consulted). Reforms with fiscal teeth all need MoFin movement.
- **Industry/Construction** appears in 5 rows. The sector that generates the biggest waste streams is also the most politically organized to resist changes.
- **Municipalities** are I on most pre-new-law policy rows but become R on three new-law rows (source sep, NWIS reporting, zone participation). The new law upgrades KOM's accountability without obviously upgrading its capacity.

## What's missing or unclear

- **Specific roles for the Constitutional Court** in striking down or upholding waste-related municipal regulations are unclear.
- **No row for hazardous/medical waste** specifically — the dossier flags this is essentially unmanaged. Fits in policy but no clear A.
- **Cross-municipal coordination** previously not modeled here is now partially captured by WMZ rows, but the relationship between zonal governance and individual KOM autonomy is under-specified in the deck.
- **HReg governance model** (board structure, appointment authority, funding source) not in the deck. Independence-from-MMPHI is the design intent but unverified.
- **Cross-Sector Coordination body** named in the deck but its membership, secretariat, and decision rights are not yet documented — not modeled as a column pending detail.
