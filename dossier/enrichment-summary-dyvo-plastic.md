## 2. Methodology stack (validates findings)

The research went through four stages:

1. **Legal analysis** — Kosovo's environmental laws + EU acquis review.
2. **Lexathon** — 2-day legal amendment marathon with law students + young professionals. Three thematic groups: monitoring responsibilities, enforcement responsibilities, preventive responsibilities. Output: proposed legal amendments to Kosovo's waste framework.
3. **Citizen focus groups** — 7 in-person meetings, one per municipality. Plus 1 civil-society meeting.
4. **Quantitative survey** — open online questionnaire, 150 respondents, snowball sampling via social media. 20 closed-ended questions + 4 open-ended. Mostly young respondents (largest cohort: 21-25).

### Institutional stakeholders interviewed (named in methodology)

- **Deputy Minister of Environment, Spatial Planning and Infrastructure** (MMPHI)
- **Representatives of the Kosovo Environmental Protection Agency** (AMMK / KEPA)
- **Director of Public Services, Municipality of Pristina** — likely Bekim Brestovci or predecessor (the dossier names Brestovci, who was the 2025 director; this 2023 research interviewed his predecessor)
- **Members of the Parliamentary Green Caucus** — **NEW actor** not in dossier or INDEP/KAS

The Parliamentary Green Caucus is worth flagging — it's a cross-party caucus on environmental issues, separate from any single ministry, and it's not in the existing dossier.

## 3. Citizen findings — the qualitative layer

### What citizens said about plastic

- **Strong concern** about plastic, especially single-use, but **uncertain about specific health risks** (microplastics in particular; participants admitted limited knowledge).
- Plastic seen as both health and **aesthetic** problem — "unpleasant to the eye" / "disfiguring natural beauty" recurring framing.
- Several explicitly proposed **plastic bag charges** based on Albania or Switzerland models — *citizens themselves suggesting policy interventions*.
- One Pristina respondent (Y.H.): *"Plastic is everywhere, and to imagine life without it would mean rethinking our lives from scratch. But what should be done with plastic is that it must be taxed properly."*
- One Gjakova respondent: *"You cannot fight plastic"* — fatalistic framing also present.

### What citizens said about local waste management

- **Urban-rural split** is the dominant axis, more than municipality-by-municipality variation. Peripheral / village areas reported reliable weekly collection. **City centers reported more problems** because of higher density + container shortage.
- **Container shortage is the #1 named operational complaint** across municipalities. Pristina respondent in *Banesat e Standardit* (Bregu i Diellit): containers too few + too far away.
- **Cross-neighborhood dumping** — Ferizaj respondents reported residents from other neighborhoods coming to dump in theirs. Suggests **bin tourism is a documented behavior**.
- **Mitrovica flooding (Jan 2023)** carried trash and debris through the Lushta River canal, exacerbating non-collection. Climate × waste interaction is real.
- **Prizren reported the fewest problems** — low density, daily collection in some areas. Comparator: collection-quality is achievable when density allows.

### What citizens said about recycling

- **Most don't recycle, most try to reuse** (especially plastic bags + bottles). When asked if they would recycle if a system existed: **all participants said yes**.
- Gjakova respondent: recycles coffee grounds for mushroom growing. Knows of a glass recycler but rarely produces glass waste.
- Gjilan respondent: separates plastic into a single pile *"so it's ready for those who need it"* — describes informal handover to scavengers in their own words.
- Peja: cited a local textile recycling organization / charity for old clothes.

**Reading:** the informal recycling sector that the dossier names (RAE scavengers) is **operationally embedded in households' practices**. Citizens separate for the scavengers, not for a formal system.

### What citizens said about enforcement

- **Almost none had been fined for waste violations.** One exception: a Mitrovica respondent fined for *deliberately* dumping waste on the street as protest after non-collection.
- Citizens **did not know where to report illegal waste.** Some guessed police; some guessed inspection services. **None had ever reported illegal dumping.**
- Awareness of environmental law: **very low.** Few had received any leaflet or seen a government commercial.
- When asked what counts as "illegal waste," citizens listed: plastic bags, combustibles, healthcare waste, construction waste, chemicals, organics outside containers, burned plastic, hazardous oils, restaurant oils into sewage. **Citizen taxonomy of "illegal waste" is roughly the EU hazardous-waste list.**

### Constitutional grounding (already in dossier but expanded)

- **Article 52** of the Kosovo Constitution: right to a healthy environment + state obligation to protect it (already in dossier)
- The DYVÓ doc additionally cites that public institutions are obligated to consider environmental impact in decision-making **and to enable citizens to participate** when those decisions concern environmental health. **This is an under-leveraged participatory right** — citizens have a constitutional claim to participation that current waste governance doesn't fulfill.

### Key principles enumerated (from Law 03/L-025)

The doc enumerates seven principles. Three matter most for the simulation:
- **Polluter pays** — the polluter bears compensation cost
- **User pays** — users of natural resources bear running-down + restoration costs
- **Public access to information** — every legal/natural person has the right to be informed about environmental state + participate in decision-making

The "polluter pays" + "user pays" combination is the legal foundation for any future Pay-as-You-Throw + EPR + landfill tax architecture.

## 7. The DYVÓ recommendations (six)

These are different from but **compatible with** the INDEP/KAS 15. DYVÓ's recommendations are more focused on plastic + commercial waste:

1. **Adopt an Administrative Instruction on prevention/reduction of single-use plastics** — concrete bans on plastic bags, bottles, straws, disposable containers.
2. **Rigorous taxation system for producers + importers of plastics** — both domestic producers and importers, no exemptions.
3. **Establish "Pay-As-You-Throw" legal framework, focus on commercial waste** — businesses charged by quantity. Households potentially included.
4. **Assign greater responsibility to businesses for waste** — businesses required to draft waste management plans naming quantity, type, disposal method. Builds on EPR principle.
5. **Implement source-separation infrastructure with incentive policies** — separate containers + processing facilities + tax reductions for compliant companies + financial incentives for compliant households.
6. **Awareness campaigns** — early environmental education in schools, media campaigns, political-actor involvement.

### Cross-mapping to INDEP/KAS 15 recommendations

| DYVÓ rec | Closest INDEP/KAS rec |
|---|---|
| 1. Single-use plastics AI | INDEP/KAS rec 3 (EPR for packaging) |
| 2. Tax on plastic producers + importers | INDEP/KAS rec 10 (landfill tax) — complementary, not duplicate |
| 3. Pay-As-You-Throw, commercial focus | INDEP/KAS rec 5 (performance-based municipal financing) — partial overlap |
| 4. Business waste responsibility | INDEP/KAS rec 3 (EPR), rec 8 (CDW recovery) |
| 5. Source separation + incentives | INDEP/KAS rec 6 (mandatory source separation) |
| 6. Awareness campaigns | INDEP/KAS rec 15 (national education + awareness program) |

DYVÓ's distinctive contribution: **#2 (plastic-specific producer + importer tax)** is more aggressive than INDEP/KAS's general EPR framing. **#3 (PAYT for commercial)** is concrete, specific to the commercial-waste problem the original dossier flagged.

## 8. Lexathon recommendations — three thematic groups

The doc's Lexathon section (Part 3) yielded three sets of legal-amendment proposals:

1. **Monitoring Responsibilities Group** — proposed amendments improving monitoring authority + capacity (specific text would be in original Albanian doc).
2. **Enforcement Responsibilities Group** — proposed amendments strengthening enforcement against illegal dumping + service failures.
3. **Preventive Responsibilities Group** — proposed amendments shifting policy upstream to prevention.

The three groups map directly to the **three layers of our enforcement RACI** (`dossier/raci/enforcement.md`). The RACI's "phantom A" finding — accountability assigned but rarely activated — is exactly what the Lexathon's enforcement group was trying to legislate around.

## 10. Implications for the hackathon's two project tracks

### For the Trash Reporter app (HackTheTrash)

- **Citizens explicitly don't know where to report illegal waste.** "Some suggested they might call the police; others said inspection services should be the right address. However, almost none had the practice of reporting illegal waste when they came across [it]." This is the gap the app fills directly.
- **The constitutional claim to participation** (Article 52 + companion provisions) gives citizen-reporting apps a strong rights-based framing.
- **Cross-neighborhood "bin tourism"** suggests the app's GPS data could expose waste-flow patterns invisible to current monitoring.

### For the Trash Management Sim

- **Citizen perception is a state variable.** DYVÓ shows citizens' awareness of policy + willingness to recycle is high but knowledge of mechanisms is low. The Sim could model "citizen-readiness" as a multiplier on policy effectiveness.
- **Container shortage** is the #1 operational complaint. **A Sim card for "expand container infrastructure" is genuinely high-leverage** — much more than its ministerial-level invisibility suggests.
- **The plastic-tax + PAYT-for-commercial recommendations** are policy levers separate from INDEP/KAS's set. **Adds depth to the policy card deck.**
- **The Lexathon process itself** is a Sim mechanic — players who hold "civil-society engagement" cards could trigger legal-amendment proposals.
