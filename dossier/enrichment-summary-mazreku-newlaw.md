## 2. The headline announcement: a new Law on Integrated Waste Management

Law 04/L-060 (2012, with amendments 2022 and 2024) is being **replaced**, not just amended. Per slide 2:

> "The Ministry of Environment, Spatial Planning and Infrastructure has initiated the drafting of a new Law on Integrated Waste Management, which is foreseen in the Government Legislative Program for 2025."

### Why a new law? (slides 3-4)

Five "current challenges" listed verbatim:
1. **Outdated legal base** (Law No. 04/L-060, 2012, with focused amendments in 2022 and 2024 related to permits and harmonization with administrative procedures)
2. **Weak provisions for Extended Producer Responsibility and Deposit Return System**
3. **Fragmented institutional roles**
4. **Lack of financial sustainability and data transparency**
5. **Heavy dependence on landfilling (≈90% of municipal waste)**

### Drafting timeline (slides 22-26)

| Phase | Date |
|---|---|
| Development and finalization of the draft law | **Nov-Dec 2025** |
| Approval by the Government | **Feb-Mar 2026** |
| Approval by the Assembly | **Aug-Sep 2026** |
| Issuance of secondary legislation (including EPR secondary legislation) | **Feb-Mar 2027** |

**Today is May 9, 2026** — we're between phases 2 (Government approval) and 3 (Assembly approval). **The law is currently in draft, has been Government-approved, and is awaiting Assembly approval.** This is an active, time-sensitive process worth tracking.

## 4. Core principles of the new law (slide 5)

Five principles, two of which are new:

| Principle | Status |
|---|---|
| **Waste hierarchy:** Prevention → Reuse → Recycling → Recovery → Disposal | Standard EU framing |
| **Polluter Pays Principle (PPP)** — producers, companies, households take financial responsibility for the waste they create | Already in 2012 law |
| **🆕 Full Net Cost Coverage (FNCC)** — collection, transport, recycling, treatment all covered by user/producer fees | **NEW** principle. This is a hard fiscal rule: no taxpayer subsidy of waste services. Major shift if enforced. |
| **Transparency and accountability** — all institutions report clearly on collect/recycle/dispose | Already in EU acquis |
| **Recycling targets of the Strategy must be achieved** | New legal teeth on existing strategy targets |

**FNCC is the load-bearing change.** It legally requires waste services to recover their costs from users — which means PAYT (Pay-As-You-Throw) and EPR fees become non-negotiable, not optional.

## 5. Institutional architecture in the new law

Slide 6 + slides 12-19 lay out the institutional framework. Several are new or expanded:

### Existing actors with expanded roles

| Actor | Current role (per dossier) | Role in new law |
|---|---|---|
| **MMPHI / MESPI** | Lead waste-policy authority | Same — leads policy, primary + secondary legislation, **PRO licensing** (NEW) |
| **KEPA / AMMK** | Environmental monitoring | **Collects and manages national waste data**, monitors compliance + reporting |
| **Inspectorate** | Loosely defined | **Ensures all operators follow law, enforces penalties** for illegal dumping/non-compliance |
| **KLMC** | Operates landfills | Same + **future regional sorting and treatment centers** (expanded scope) |
| **Municipalities** | Local collection | Same + **cooperate with other municipalities in shared waste zones** (NEW: zonal cooperation legally required) |

### NEW institutional actors

| Actor | Role |
|---|---|
| **🆕 Hybrid Regulator** | "For tariff setting, performance monitoring and dispute resolution." Doesn't currently exist. **This is the independent regulator the dossier flagged as missing** (analogous to ARRU for water, ERO for energy). |
| **🆕 Producer Responsibility Organizations (PROs)** | Now have a strong legal basis. EPR schemes for packaging, WEEE, batteries, ELV, tyres, "other waste streams." |
| **🆕 Cross-Sector Coordination** body | Coordinates policies + investments; includes central + local institutions, private sector, civil society, donors. **Recommendation 2 from INDEP/KAS now has a name and a slide.** |
| **🆕 Five Waste Management Zones** | Inter-municipal cooperation legally enshrined. Five zones to be defined. **This is structurally new** — overrides per-municipality fragmentation. |

### Private sector

Slide 17 specifies four private-sector roles:
1. Producers/companies take responsibility through EPR Schemes
2. Recycling companies + PROs manage collection, sorting, recycling
3. Investors/innovators develop new recycling plants, repair/reuse businesses, digital waste tracking services
4. **PPPs (public-private partnerships) preferred** to engage experienced EU operators for modern infrastructure

## 6. EPR + DRS specifics

### Extended Producer Responsibility scope (slide 7)

EPR will cover:
- Packaging
- WEEE (electronic waste)
- Batteries
- End-of-life vehicles (ELV)
- Tyres
- "Other waste streams"

### Deposit Refund System scope (slide 7)

DRS will cover:
- **Plastic, glass, and aluminum bottles**
- **Range: 0.15 to 2 liters**
- Goal: increase collection + recycling rates, reduce littering nationwide

This is **more specific than the INDEP/KAS framing**, which said "for plastic, glass, metal beverage packaging." The 0.15-2L range is the operational scope.

### EPR + DRS development timeline (slides 7-10)

| Year | Milestone |
|---|---|
| 2023 | EPR Assessments for WEE, Batteries, ELVs, Oils |
| 2026 | Legal base for EPR introduced into the New Law on Integrated Waste Management; EPR assessments for Tyres and Packaging |
| 2027 | New Secondary Legislation including EPR adopted |

The 2023 "EPR Assessments" predate the law — these are *preparatory studies*, not operating systems.

## 7. Data, monitoring, enforcement (slide 11)

Four planned elements:
1. **Establishment of a National Waste Information System (NWIS)** — INDEP/KAS Recommendation 1 has a name now.
2. **Mandatory reporting** by municipalities, operators, and PROs.
3. **Strengthened inspection powers.**
4. **Regular performance and compliance audits.**

The whole "phantom A" enforcement layer flagged in `dossier/raci/enforcement.md` is being addressed in the new law architecturally — though only secondary legislation in 2027 will determine whether it actually gains teeth.

## 8. Recycling targets — the 50% number

Slide 20 has a target breakdown that totals to a **50% recycling rate**:

| Stream / mechanism | Share |
|---|---|
| Sorting Centre / MBT (tunnel) / Recycling Market | **24.8% Recycling** |
| Anaerobic Digestion / Composting | **9.5% Recycling** |
| Landfill | **20% Landfilled** |
| RDF/WtE | **6.3% Recycling** (waste-to-energy treated as recycling here, EU-disputed) |
| Anaerobic Digestion (separate) | **6.2% Recycling** |
| Other | **3.3% Recycling** |
| **Total Recycling** | **~50%** |

**This is a 47-percentage-point jump from the current ~3% recycling rate.** Whether achievable by 2030 or 2035 will depend on:
- DRS launching (still unverified — issue #4)
- EPR systems operating (PROs don't exist yet)
- MRF infrastructure built (currently zero national sorting plants)
- Source separation mandate enforced (~5% bio-waste currently separated)

The ~50% target matches EU average recycling rate (44% per INDEP/KAS) — Kosovo is targeting *EU parity* by the strategy's end date.

Operational source-separation scheme described:

|---|---|
| **Apartment blocks** | 3-bin system |
| **Individual houses** | 2-bin system |

Streams covered:
- Plastic
- Paper
- Glass (in dedicated bins)
- Recyclable Waste (mixed recyclables)
- Mix (residual)
- Green Waste (urban areas — public spaces / parks)

## 11. Implications for the hackathon's two project tracks

### For the Trash Reporter app (HackTheTrash)

- **The new law mandates a National Waste Information System (NWIS)**. The Reporter app could be designed as **a citizen-facing front-end to NWIS** — making citizen reports a structured input to the state's data infrastructure rather than a parallel system.
