## Pre-2016: Baseline

The legal and physical infrastructure that the rest of the timeline runs on top of. Most of these foundations are still load-bearing today.

- **2001-2008** — All seven public landfills built with EU support (Mirash, Gërmova, Sferkë, Landovicë, Velekincë, Podujevë, Dragash). All have since exceeded designed capacity; none possess leachate treatment. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2004-2006** — Mirash landfill constructed at ~€3.5M, on KEK-owned land in Obiliq, in a former coal pit. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2006-12** — Mirash landfill begins operating (December 2006). *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2008** — World Bank Strategic Environmental and Social Assessment published; cited later by INDEP/KAS for industrial waste volumes (1M+ t/yr power plant ash; 60M t Trepça mining waste accumulated historically). *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2010-2020** — Original Kosovo waste management strategy in force. Set 100% waste collection target by 2020; actual achievement was ~85% households / 54% businesses. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2012** — Administrative Instruction 19/2012 on end-of-life vehicles adopted. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2012** — Administrative Instruction 15/2012 on landfill management adopted. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2012-05-24** — **Kosovo Waste Law No. 04/L-060** adopted. National-level statute governing waste management; widely assessed as inadequate for enforcement. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`, `enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2013** — European Commission cites Law 04/L-060 as an example of how Kosovo's laws lack enforcement capacity. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2014** — Total Kosovo waste generation: 247,000 tonnes (AMMK Environmental Indicators 2018). *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2014** — Administrative Instruction 29/2014 on sludge from polluted water treatment adopted. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2014** — Administrative Instruction 26/2014 on battery + accumulator waste adopted. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2014** — Administrative Instruction 27/2014 on packaging + wrapping waste adopted. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2014** — Administrative Instruction 25/2014 on WEEE adopted. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2014** — Administrative Instruction 21/2014 on mining/extractive industry waste adopted. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2015** — Administrative Instruction 05/2015 on PCB/PCT polychlorinated biphenyls adopted. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2015** — Administrative Instruction 15/2015 on mercury-containing fluorescent tubes adopted. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2015** — Mirash landfill receiving ~100,000 tons/year (per 2017 Prishtina Insight reporting on this period). *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*

## 2016-2019: Pre-MPG-CE Baseline

The "before" picture against which the GIZ Municipal Performance Grant scheme would later be measured.

- **2016** — Waste service coverage: 54%. *(`enrichment-summary-pg-ce.md`)*
- **2016** — Administrative Instruction 06/2016 on C&D landfill site selection adopted. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2016-2020** — Construction & demolition waste accumulated ~863,000 tonnes (MMPHI Roadmap 2023 figure). *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2017** — Waste service coverage: 70.9%. Fee collection rate: 80.64%. Only 3 of 38 municipalities reporting annually to KEPA/AMMK. *(`enrichment-summary-pg-ce.md`)*
- **2017** — Illegal landfills nationally: **2,246**. *(`enrichment-summary-pg-ce.md`)*
- **2017-05-29** — Prishtina Insight publishes detailed Mirash investigation: groundwater pollution, leachate plan failed within one year of opening, no degassing system, Ombudsperson Hilmi Jashari warns of methane explosion risk akin to 1993 Turkey landfill disaster. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2018** — Total Kosovo waste generation: 284,000 tonnes (AMMK Environmental Indicators 2018). *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2018** — Per AMMK: Kosovo had 2,246 illegal landfills (figure carried into later reports). *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2019** — Reçica & Köcker publish "The Plastics Industry Value Chain in Kosovo" via MINT. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2019-12-10** — Ombudsperson Jashari leads environmental march in Pristina; comments that prosecution and courts do not prioritize environmental cases. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*

## 2020-2022: MPG-CE — Kosovo's Working Performance-Funding Example

The €7M Municipal Performance Grant — Clean Environment scheme. The single best documented example of policy intervention producing measurable system response in the dossier.

- **2020** — MPG-CE program launches: €7M total, 2020-2022, owned by Kosovo Government, led by MMPHI/MESPI. Targeted all 38 municipalities; 24 (~63%) eventually awarded. *(`enrichment-summary-pg-ce.md`)*
- **2020** — Pristina-region waste collection +2.2% vs 2019; other regions +0.5% (KAS Municipal Waste Survey 2021). *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2021** — Joint AMMK/GIZ study identifies ~1,572 illegal dumpsites across 38 municipalities. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2021** — Waste service coverage: 90.2%. Fee collection rate: 81.80%. **35 of 38 municipalities now reporting annually to KEPA/AMMK** (up from 3 in 2017). *(`enrichment-summary-pg-ce.md`)*
- **2021-05** — MMPHI adopts the Kosovo Integrated Waste Management Strategy 2021-2030. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2022** — Illegal landfills nationally: **763** (down from 2,246 in 2017 — ~66% decline during MPG-CE). *(`enrichment-summary-pg-ce.md`)*
- **2022** — MPG-CE program closes. Equipment delivered across 24 municipalities: 23 collection trucks, 4 skipper trucks, 11 sweeping machines, 3 tractors, 16,400 containers (3,000 × 240L + 11,000 × 120L + 2,400 × 1.1m³), 3,500 home composters, 70,000+ new households added to formal collection, 30,162 households in 17 municipalities with separate collection infrastructure. *(`enrichment-summary-pg-ce.md`)*
- **2022** — NPISSA 2022-2026 (National Programme for Implementation of the Stabilisation and Association Agreement) adopted; items 32 & 33 cover waste-management infrastructure and awareness. *(`enrichment-summary-pg-ce.md`)*
- **2022** — Law 08/L-071 adopted, amending Law on Waste 04/L-060 (focused on permits and harmonization with administrative procedures). *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`, `enrichment-summary-mazreku-newlaw.md`)*

## 2023: DYVÓ Research Period — Citizen Baseline

DYVÓ NGO's mixed-methods research across 7 municipalities. Captures pre-crisis citizen perceptions, before the December 2024 Pastrimi-Komuna regulation amendment.

- **2023-01** — Mitrovica flooding event carries trash and debris through the Lushta River canal; non-collection compounds the impact. Documented climate × waste interaction. *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2023** — BECBA bio-waste study completed: only ~5% of bio-waste in Kosovo separated/treated. *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2023** — GIZ packaging-reuse study completed. *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2023** — EPR Assessments completed for WEEE, Batteries, ELVs, Oils (preparatory studies, not operating systems). *(`enrichment-summary-mazreku-newlaw.md`)*
- **2023** — MMPHI publishes Roadmap for Circular Economy (Udhërrëfyesi për Ekonominë Qarkore). *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2023** — Total municipal waste collected: 471,278 tonnes; per-capita generation 240 kg/yr; service coverage 93.6%; fee collection 89%; planning compliance 66%; illegal dumpsites 403 (AMMK 2025 report). *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2023** — Kosovo ranked 3rd worst country globally for unsorted household waste (Lloyd's Register Foundation World Risk Poll). *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2023** — Kosovo GDP: €9.7 billion. *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2023-03 / 2023-04 / 2023-05** — DYVÓ research conducted: legal analysis + Lexathon (2-day legal-amendment marathon) + 7 municipal focus groups (Pristina, Prizren, Peja, Gjakova, Gjilan, Ferizaj, Mitrovica) + 150-respondent online quantitative survey + institutional stakeholder interviews (Deputy Minister MMPHI, AMMK reps, Pristina Director of Public Services, Parliamentary Green Caucus members). *(`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*
- **2023-06** — Pastrimi reportedly completes a registration update and submits ~25,000 new clients to the Municipality of Pristina for the registry. The Municipality does not act on this submission — origin of the eventual 23,000-client gap. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2023-09-01** — Prishtina Insight publishes "Waste Warriors of Necessity" — feature on RAE informal waste pickers. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2023-12-01** — Pristina's Waste Management Regulation No. 01-030/01-227581/23 in force (the regulation that will be amended a year later). *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **(year unspecified in source)** — Plastic-bag tariff Administrative Order 04/2025 in development during the DYVÓ research period; framing the citizen-suggested "tax it properly" interventions. ⚠ Internal source inconsistency: Order numbered 04/2025 cannot have caused 2023→2024 import drop; verify against underlying admin-order text. *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`, `enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md`)*

## 2024: Strategy + Concept Document + Pastrimi-Komuna Regulation Crisis Begins

The year the policy stack moves and the Pristina operational system fractures.

- **2024** — Total municipal waste collected: 449,708 tonnes; per-capita generation 294 kg/yr; service coverage 90.9%; fee collection 90.4%; planning compliance 65%; illegal dumpsites 458 (AMMK 2025 report). *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2024** — Plastic bag imports drop **−68.4%** (measured 2023 → 2024 per AMMK). ⚠ Source attributes drop to Administrative Order 04/2025 — see internal inconsistency note above. *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2024** — Pastrimi estimates Municipality's failure to register clients cost the company €3.5M in foregone revenue during the year. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2024** — Municipality of Pristina issues a tender for five private waste operators, intended to break Pastrimi's de facto monopoly. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2024-03-29** — Balkan Insight / BIRN publishes regional illegal-landfills mapping ("It's Devastating"). *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2024-04** — Strategy and Action Plan for Integrated Waste Management 2024-2030 revised (the version cited by INDEP/KAS). *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`, `enrichment-summary-mazreku-newlaw.md`)*
- **2024** — MMPHI Strategy 2024-2030: Kosovo Action Plan 2024-2026 (waste + CE) committed at **€41M**; total Kosovo CE investments planned **€77M** through 2030. *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2024-09-17** — Balkan Insight: Kosovo, Montenegro, Albania ranked among world's worst in household waste disposal. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2024-10** — Concept Document on Integrated Waste Management Reform endorsed (predecessor to the new Law). *(`enrichment-summary-mazreku-newlaw.md`)*
- **2024-11-22** — RFE/RL publishes "The Ticking Trash Time Bomb in the Balkans"; KLMC director Dardan Velija on Mirash sewage flooding "out of control for many years." *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2024** — Deposit Refund System (DRS / SRD) secondary legislation passed in 2024 per INDEP/KAS framing. ⚠ Tension with Mazreku deck which shows DRS legal base coming in 2026 with new Law — verification still open. *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`, `enrichment-summary-mazreku-newlaw.md`)*
- **2024-12** — Integrated Waste Management Strategy **2024-2035** approved by the Government per Mazreku slide 4. ⚠ Strategy date discrepancy with INDEP/KAS's 2024-2030 framing — see [`tensions.md`](tensions.md). *(`enrichment-summary-mazreku-newlaw.md`)*
- **2024-12-05** — Pristina Municipal Assembly votes to amend the waste management regulation (PDK + VV in favor; LDK opposes). The amendment grants Pastrimi exclusive billing authority and effectively cancels the Municipality's private operator tender. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2024-12-06** — Amendment recorded as Regulation No. 01-030/01-161704/24, modifying the December 2023 base regulation. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*

## 2025: Crisis Year

The Pastrimi-Komuna dispute moves from regulation to billing chaos to strikes to court ruling, all in roughly six months.

- **2025-01** — DRS planned launch (per INDEP/KAS framing of Administrative Order 04/2025). ⚠ Verification open; Mazreku deck implies later. *(`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md`)*
- **2025-01-01** — Pastrimi begins billing citizens directly under the new regulation. The Municipality continues issuing its own waste bills and refuses to recognize Pastrimi's authority. Citizens receive duplicate bills. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-01-18** — KOHA reports "Pristina residents will be billed twice for waste"; Bekim Brestovci (Director of Public Services, Municipality) apologizes to citizens for the confusion. Municipality 2025 slogan: "Prishtina the cleanest city in the region." *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-02-05** — Municipality of Pristina formally announces (via Facebook + Telegrafi/Cna.al coverage) that citizens are not obligated to pay Pastrimi invoices issued from January 1, 2025; directs them to pay the Municipality instead. Pastrimi calls the announcement "illegal and arbitrary." *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-02 (mid)** — Pastrimi halts collection for several days in protest; alleges €2.7M owed by Municipality. Trash accumulates on Pristina streets. Municipality declares a state of emergency in waste collection citing "significant risk of a public health crisis." *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-03-17** — Prishtina Insight publishes "Waste Management: A Systemic Crisis in Kosovo" (Verone Zymberi & Ardita Zeqiri) — the most comprehensive English-language synthesis. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-04 (early)** — Over 21,000 citizens have made payments to the Municipality rather than Pastrimi. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-04** — Pastrimi alleges debt has reached €2M (early April), then €2.2M (end of April). *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-04-20** — KOHA: "Citizens in Pristina continue to be in a dilemma about where to pay." *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-04-26** — Telegrafi publishes extended Reçica interview framing the dispute as the Municipality attempting to replace Pastrimi with private operators. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-04-29** — KOHA: KRM Pastrimi statement says debt has reached €2.2M. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-05** — Pastrimi workers begin month-long strike, protesting both the Municipality's withholding of payments and the contracting of private operators. The Municipality hires two private companies during the strike, "but they also failed to do the job properly." *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-05-19** — Ombudsperson Hilmi Jashari convenes meeting between Pastrimi (CEO Reçica) and Municipality (Mayor Rama). Reçica attends; Rama does not. Jashari publicly criticizes the Municipality, notes Pastrimi is "on the verge of bankruptcy" and that the situation endangers public health. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-06 (early)** — Supreme Court of Kosovo rules in favor of the December 2024 regulation; rejects Municipality of Pristina's claim to repeal it. Regulation stands; Pastrimi has billing authority. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-06-24** — Even with Supreme Court ruling and mediation underway, Pastrimi workers protest in front of company building, opposing continued operation of the private operators. Worker Adnan Sinani quoted to KOHA. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-06-25** — Municipality of Pristina, through mediation, returns the debt of collected funds to Pastrimi following the Supreme Court ruling. Worker strike ends. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025 late** — Mayor Përparim Rama (LDK) wins runoff election but as of January 2026 has not yet presented his governing cabinet. Regulation stands. Pastrimi bills directly. Private-operator tender not formally cancelled. KLMC debt situation continues (Pastrimi owes ~€1.9M). Mirash continues operating beyond capacity. *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-11** — Late 2025: New Law on Integrated Waste Management — drafting phase begins (Nov-Dec 2025). *(`enrichment-summary-mazreku-newlaw.md`)*
- **2025-11-28** — Balkan Insight publishes "Unbearable: The Legal and Illegal Landfills Harming Lives and Land in Kosovo." *(`how-trash-works-pristina.md`)*
- **2025-12** — New Law drafting finalization (Nov-Dec 2025 phase). *(`enrichment-summary-mazreku-newlaw.md`)*

## Notable Trajectories

The time series across the dossier sources, useful for sizing change.

### Waste service coverage (population)

| Year | Coverage | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 2016 | **54%** | GIZ MPG-CE deck |
| 2017 | 70.9% | GIZ MPG-CE deck |
| 2021 | **90.2%** | GIZ MPG-CE deck (post-MPG-CE) |
| 2023 | **93.6%** | AMMK 2025 / INDEP/KAS |
| 2024 | 90.9% | AMMK 2025 / INDEP/KAS |

Reading: ~36-percentage-point jump 2016 → 2021 substantially attributable to the MPG-CE €7M scheme. Slight regression 2023 → 2024 suggests gains held but momentum stalled when MPG-CE ended.

### Illegal landfills (count)

| Year | Count | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 2017 | **2,246** | GIZ MPG-CE deck (matches AMMK historical) |

| 2023 | **403** | AMMK 2025 |
| 2024 | 458 | AMMK 2025 |

Reading: ~80% decline 2017 → 2023. Small uptick 2023 → 2024. Civil society (Let's Do It Kosovo) currently asserts 500+; methodology gap explains the spread.
