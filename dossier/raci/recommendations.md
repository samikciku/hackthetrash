## Row notes

**Row 1 — Material flow data.** Foundational; everything else depends on it. AMMK already runs environmental databases — they're the operational R. The recommendation is to expand into material flows specifically. Currently rated as fragmented data systems by INDEP/KAS itself.

**Row 2 — Inter-Ministerial CE Coordination Unit.** Doesn't exist. Office of the Prime Minister (CG) would be the establishment R via government decision; MMPHI is the policy A. **High-leverage card** because so many other recommendations depend on cross-ministerial coordination.

**Row 3 — EPR for priority streams.** PRO column is "R to-form" — the Producer Responsibility Organizations don't exist yet; they're created when EPR is operationalized. Industry/producers are C because EPR shifts cost onto them.

**Row 4 — DRS rollout.** Already has secondary legislation (2024); planned launch January 2025. Status unverified (issue #4). MMPHI is A,R for the system; PROs operate (R ? until verified).

**Row 5 — Performance-based municipal financing.** Tie municipal transfers to recycling + landfill diversion outcomes. MoFin is the operational R (controls the transfers); MMPHI is the policy A. Currently doesn't exist — funding is not performance-linked.

**Row 6 — Source separation mandate.** MMPHI sets the rule; municipalities operate; **citizens are the unrecognized R** — separation only works if households actually separate. Citizens listed as R captures this.

**Row 7 — Investment program.** MoFin allocates; the EU's Reform & Growth Facility (€6B 2024-2027) is the largest funding pool. **Donor R via PPP** (public-private partnership) is the implementation pattern.

**Row 8 — C&D waste recovery.** Construction sector is **R (must comply)**. This is the single biggest waste stream by volume; non-compliance is the documented largest illegal-dumping driver. Hard cell to fill — the actor most resistant to the rule is the actor who has to do it.

**Row 9 — Industrial symbiosis platform.** MINT is the lead institution per the doc. Industry participates (R). Doesn't exist yet. Trepça lead-battery recycling is the closest existing example (verification issue #5).

**Row 10 — Landfill tax.** MoFin owns it (legislative amendment + revenue). Doesn't exist yet. UK comparison: ~£126/ton; Ireland ~€80/ton. INDEP/KAS proposes gradual introduction to avoid spiking illegal dumping.

**Row 11 — Reuse + repair centers.** Municipalities operate; civil society is also R (NGOs run reuse centers in many comparison cities). Citizens are R because the model only works if they actually use the centers.

**Row 12 — Green Public Procurement.** Public Procurement Regulatory Commission (under MoFin) is A,R. Currently weak. Industry is C as supplier base.

**Row 13 — Strengthen enforcement vs illegal dumping.** Municipal inspectors are R; MMPHI is A. **Citizens are also R via reporting** — and the Trash Reporter app (HackTheTrash) is exactly the citizen-side tool the recommendation calls for.

**Row 14 — Financial schemes for CE innovation.** Donors are R (funding + grants); MoFin is A. The EU's RGF is the biggest pool. PROs not directly involved.

**Row 15 — Education + awareness.** Ministry of Education has the operational R for curriculum; MMPHI is policy A. Citizens are R as the audience whose behavior is meant to change.

**Reading:** MMPHI is the load-bearing institution for over half the recommendations. Its capacity is the binding constraint. **MoFin appears in any recommendation with fiscal teeth** — landfill tax, GPP, CE innovation finance, performance-based municipal funding. **No recommendation has MINT or MoFin as a clean A,R.** Industrial-policy and fiscal-policy reforms are split between A and R, suggesting weaker alignment than the strict environmental-policy rows.

## Sequencing implications for the Sim

If players treat these as cards, several rows are dependent:

- **Row 1 (data system)** unlocks **Row 5 (performance-based funding)** — can't reward outcomes without data.
- **Row 2 (Coordination Unit)** unlocks **Rows 3, 6, 7, 8** — cross-ministerial work needs the unit.
- **Row 3 (EPR)** unlocks **Row 4 (DRS operation)** — DRS is a specific EPR implementation.
- **Row 10 (landfill tax)** is a revenue source for **Row 7 (investment)** and **Row 14 (innovation finance)**.

Suggested play order in a Sim: Row 1 → Row 2 → Rows 5+10 in parallel → 3+4 → 7+8+11+13 → 6+9+12 → 14+15.

## What's missing or unclear

- **PRO actors** are written into 5 rows but operationally don't exist yet. Big paper architecture gap.
- **Cross-municipal coordination** between Pristina and surrounding municipalities (sharing Mirash) isn't a row; could be added.
- **Constitutional Court / Supreme Court** review of waste-related legislation isn't surfaced as a separate enforcement node. Any of these recommendations needing legislative changes (rows 8, 10, 12) could be challenged in court.
- **Specific timeline** for each recommendation: INDEP/KAS proposes "implementation by 2030" overall but doesn't sequence them. The dependency analysis above is mine, not the document's.
