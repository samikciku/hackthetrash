# Operational RACI — Collection, Billing, Landfill

> **Two time horizons.** This matrix now spans **pre-new-law (current state, 2024-2026)** and **post-new-law (2027+)** activities. The Pastrimi-Komuna-KLMC dispute layer is current-state; new rows tagged **(new-law)** describe operational activities introduced by the new Law on Integrated Waste Management (Government approval Feb-Mar 2026, Assembly approval Aug-Sep 2026, secondary legislation Feb-Mar 2027 — per Mazreku/MMPHI deck). A new structural actor — **Five Waste Management Zones (WMZ)** — only becomes operational post-2027. Rows tagged **(post-2024-extraction)** describe behaviors documented in the 2023 DYVÓ research that have no clean accountability today.

> Layer 1 of 3. Where the 2024-2025 Pastrimi-Komuna-KLMC dispute lives. ~85% confident on pre-new-law rows — the dispute is well-documented, but registry numbers and tipping-fee debt remain contested between the actors themselves. ~60% confident on new-law rows — sourced from the Mazreku deck and describe institutions/facilities that don't yet exist.

## Actors in this matrix

KOM · **WMZ** · ASMB · PAS · PRV · KLM · MoEcon · MMPHI · INSP · COURT · OMB · CIT

**New actor codes (introduced by new Law on Integrated Waste Management):**

- **WMZ — Five Waste Management Zones.** Inter-municipal cooperation legally enshrined in the new law: Kosovo's municipalities will be grouped into five waste management zones for shared infrastructure (CACs, MRFs, transfer stations, landfills) and joint operational planning. Zones operate the shared backbone; KOM operate within and participate. Operational from 2027. Until then, WMZ cells read "—" for current-state rows.
- **INSP — Inspectorate.** Existing actor surfaced explicitly here for new-law / DYVÓ-flagged rows where enforcement is the live question. Per Mazreku deck, the new law strengthens INSP's role for illegal dumping and operator non-compliance.

## Row notes

**Row 1 — Pass municipal waste regulation.** The Dec 2024 Regulation No. 01-030/01-161704/24 was passed by ASMB (PDK + VV bloc) over Mayor Rama's (LDK) objection. KOM as executive opposes; ASMB is the A,R. MMPHI formally signed off on the amendment as legally compliant — that's an "I (approves)" role.

**Row 2 — Set tariff.** Flat €6.20/mo with VAT. Pastrimi sets the rate; the Assembly authorizes. Both claim authority; KOM disputes it post-Dec 2024. ⚠ flag captures the live dispute. Note: under the new law, FNCC + Hybrid Regulator (HReg) take over tariff-setting accountability — see `policy.md` row 18.

**Row 3 — Maintain client registry.** The flashpoint. Pastrimi count: ~95k clients. KOM count: ~72k. Pastrimi alleges KOM has refused to register ~25k new clients since 2023, costing ~€3.5M/year. Both ⚠ — neither side is functionally executing this.

**Row 4 — Bill citizens.** Status changed mid-2025. Before June: chaotic — both sides billed, ~21k citizens paid Komuna instead of Pastrimi by April. After Supreme Court ruling June 2025: Pastrimi has clean R; KOM forced to return collected funds via mediation June 25. Private operators billed during Feb-Jun 2025 emergency window — ⚠.

**Row 7 — Tipping fees.** Pastrimi owes KLMC ~€1.9M. Both sides have leverage (KLMC could refuse trucks; Pastrimi pays KLMC's revenue) — mutual hostage situation. ⚠.

**Row 11 — Pastrimi Board appointment.** Whiteboard finding: Pastrimi CEO is appointed by a 5-7 person Board, which is appointed by the State (specifically Min of Economy as the operator's parent ministry). This means **the shareholder (Komuna) has no operational control over the company they "own."** Major structural finding.

**Row 12 — Resolve disputes.** Supreme Court ruled in Pastrimi's favor June 2025 (R). Constitutional Court referral happened but ruling status unclear in available sources — flagged as ? for follow-up. Under the new law, in-sector disputes route to the Hybrid Regulator (post-2027) — see `policy.md` row 21.

**Row 15 — (new-law) Civic Amenity Centers (CACs).** New facilities for bulky/special waste drop-off referenced by the Mazreku deck (slide 20) as part of the household-facing source-separation architecture. Per the GIZ MPG-CE / CE-PG pitch, CACs are explicitly listed as an infrastructure type to be built under the proposed €20M CE-PG (2024-2027) and would be **shared zonally** rather than built per-municipality. KOM operates within its perimeter; WMZ operates the cross-municipal shared-CAC layer; KLMC consulted on integration with regional infrastructure; citizens are R as users (drop-off behavior is the whole point of the facility).

**Row 16 — (new-law) Materials Recovery Facilities (MRFs).** Sorting plants for separated streams. **None currently exist in Kosovo** — confirmed by GIZ deck and DYVÓ. Listed in the CE-PG infrastructure menu. KLMC is the natural A,R as the existing public landfill/treatment operator with regional scope; WMZ operates zonal MRFs as part of the shared backbone; Pastrimi is C as the upstream collection operator delivering material; MMPHI informed via NWIS reporting. The structural absence today is exactly why the row is included — the matrix should make the gap visible.

**Row 17 — (new-law) Coordinate within Five Waste Management Zones.** WMZ as the new structural actor is A,R for cross-municipal operational coordination (joint procurement, shared infrastructure, zonal planning). KOM is R for participating (joining the zone they are assigned to per the legal designation). MMPHI consulted on policy framework. This row is the operational complement to `policy.md` row 20 (where MMPHI is A,R for *establishing* the zones).

**Row 18 — Cross-neighborhood "bin tourism."** Documented by DYVÓ across multiple municipalities — Ferizaj respondents specifically reported residents from other neighborhoods crossing in to dump in their bins. **No clean A exists today.** KOM is marked ⚠ as the phantom A — accountability is implicitly with the municipality but functionally nobody enforces it. INSP is R *when* fines are applied, but DYVÓ found citizens "almost none had been fined for waste violations" and "did not know where to report illegal waste." Citizens are I (affected, not in the loop). The row is included to make the enforcement gap legible — a finding from DYVÓ that the existing matrix didn't capture.

**Row 19 — Climate × waste events.** Mitrovica's Lushta River flooding in January 2023 carried trash and debris through the city's canal, exacerbating non-collection (DYVÓ). Emergency response is **not formalized** — KOM is marked ⚠ R because the municipality is the de facto first responder but there is no documented emergency-response protocol assigning the A. KLMC is I (downstream landfill receives whatever is recovered). Citizens are I (affected residents). The row documents the ad-hoc nature of the climate-event response; expect this to become a more frequent failure mode.

## Dispute markers — concentration

- Rows 2, 3, 4, 7 all have ⚠ cells. Same cluster: tariff/registry/billing/debt. **The system's conflict is concentrated in one quadrant**, not spread across all activities.
- Rows 5, 6, 8 (actual operations: trucks, landfill) have clean A,R cells. The mechanical work isn't disputed; only the money flows are.
- Rows 18, 19 are a *different* concentration: enforcement + emergency-response gaps where ⚠ flags the absence of a clean A. These are post-2024-extraction findings from DYVÓ — same "phantom accountability" pattern as row 9, applied to enforcement and climate.

## What's missing or unclear

- **No A on row 9** (build replacement landfill). EU has tagged Mirash for closure ~10 years ago; funding studies exist; nobody is meaningfully accountable. This is exactly the kind of "phantom accountability" the system runs on.
- **Row 14** (citizen complaints) has overlapping R but no clean A — citizens report to multiple channels with no obligation on either side to respond.
- **Rows 15, 16** describe facilities that don't yet exist. CACs are referenced in the Mazreku deck and CE-PG menu but not yet built; MRFs are explicitly zero in Kosovo today. The A,R assignments are forward-looking.
- **Row 17** (zonal coordination) has WMZ as A,R but WMZ is paper architecture — five zones legally enshrined post-2027, no operational entities yet.
- **Row 18** (bin tourism) — the absence of enforcement is the finding. INSP's role activates only when fines are issued, which DYVÓ found is functionally never.
- **Row 19** (climate response) — no documented protocol for waste-system response to flooding/extreme weather. As climate events intensify, this gap will widen.
