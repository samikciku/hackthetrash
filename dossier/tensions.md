## ⚠ Tensions to resolve

### Tension 1: Service coverage figures disagree

| Source | Coverage figure | Year |
|---|---|---|
| Original dossier (Heinrich Böll Stiftung 2022; Prishtina Insight 2025) | 50-85% (range, varying by methodology — 85% households, 54% businesses, 85% public institutions) | 2022/2023 |
| **INDEP/KAS 2026 (citing AMMK Report 2025)** | **93.6% (2023) → 90.9% (2024)** | 2023-2024 |

**Reading:** Both numbers are plausibly correct for different denominators. Heinrich Böll Stiftung likely measured rural+urban combined; AMMK's 93.6% is the official agency figure and probably leans urban + formal. Treat the AMMK number as the *official* national figure and the lower numbers as the *experienced* coverage including rural gaps. The dossier should note the range exists and which authority cited which figure.

### Tension 2: Total municipal waste figure

| Source | Tonnage | Year |
|---|---|---|
| Original dossier | ~490,000 tonnes/year | 2023 |
| **INDEP/KAS 2026 (citing AMMK)** | **471,278 tonnes (2023), 449,708 tonnes (2024)** | 2023-2024 |

**Reading:** INDEP/KAS is more recent and cites AMMK directly. Use 471k (2023) / 450k (2024) going forward. The dossier's 490k was probably an extrapolated or pre-revised figure.

### Tension 3: Per-capita generation

| Source | kg/person/year | Year |
|---|---|---|
| Original dossier | 0.73 kg/person/day → ~266 kg/year | 2023 |
| **INDEP/KAS 2026** | **240 kg (2023), 294 kg (2024)** | 2023-2024 |

**Reading:** 240 (2023) is lower than the dossier's implicit 266; 294 (2024) is higher. The trend is **rising**, which the dossier didn't flag — generation is climbing as incomes rise. Use the INDEP/KAS series.

## ➕ Major additions to the picture

The original dossier was light on **policy stack** and **measurable outcomes**. INDEP/KAS adds:

### Policy stack now in force or imminent

- **Strategy and Action Plan for Integrated Waste Management 2024-2030** (revised April 2024)
- **Roadmap for Circular Economy** (MMPHI, 2023)
- **Action Plan 2024-2026** with **€41 million** committed
- **Total CE investments planned through 2030: €77 million**
- **Administrative Order 04/2025** — plastic bag tariffs, took effect 2025
- **Deposit Refund System (SRD/DRS)** — secondary legislation passed 2024, **rollout planned January 2025** (need to verify if launched on time)

### One measurable policy win

- **Plastic bag imports dropped 68.4%** between 2023 and 2024 after the Administrative Order. The system is movable. This is a load-bearing data point for any reform argument.

### Industrial waste streams the dossier underweighted

- **Construction & demolition waste:** ~863,000 tonnes generated 2016-2020. The dossier mentioned ~167,900 t/year from MMPHI — likely a different measurement window. INDEP/KAS confirms C&D is the largest single stream.
- **Thermal power plant ash:** >1 million tonnes/year (dossier missed this entirely)
- **Trepça mining complex accumulated waste:** ~60 million tonnes (dossier missed this entirely)

### Resource productivity gap (a strong economic argument)

- Western Balkans: **€0.35/kg** of material consumed
- EU average: **€2.07/kg**
- **6× gap** — reframes the circular economy from "environmental concern" to "economic catch-up." Worth using when pitching the system to decision-makers.

### EU policy context

The dossier mentioned EU accession pressure but didn't catalogue the policy stack. INDEP/KAS adds:
- EU CEAP 2020, ESPR 2024, Digital Product Passport, EU Battery Regulation, Packaging & Packaging Waste Regulation, EU Sustainable Textiles Strategy, Circular Electronics Initiative, Acquis Chapter 27, Green Agenda for Western Balkans 2020 (Kosovo committed), EU Reform & Growth Facility 2024-2027 (€6 billion)
