## ✅ Resolved tensions

### Tension 1: Service coverage figures — RESOLVED

The GIZ MPG-CE deck (`enrichment-summary-pg-ce.md`) provides the missing time series that reconciles the dossier's 50-85% range with INDEP/KAS's 93.6% headline figure. Both are correct at different time points:

| Year | Coverage | Source |
|---|---|---|
| 2016 | 54% | GIZ MPG-CE |
| 2017 | 70.9% | GIZ MPG-CE |
| 2021 | 90.2% | GIZ MPG-CE |
| 2023 | 93.6% | AMMK (via INDEP/KAS) |
| 2024 | 90.9% | AMMK (via INDEP/KAS) |

**Reading:** Coverage rose steeply between 2016 and 2021 (roughly +36 points in five years), driven by MPG-CE programme expansion. The dossier's 50-85% figures captured the earlier portion of that climb; INDEP/KAS's 93.6% is the post-expansion plateau. The slight 2023→2024 dip (93.6% → 90.9%) is real and worth tracking, but the long arc is dramatically upward. Use the time series rather than any single number.

## ⚠ Tensions to resolve

### NOT a tension: Different metrics — generation vs. collection

| Source | Metric | Figure | Year |
|---|---|---|---|
| Original dossier | Total municipal waste **generation** | ~490,000 t/yr | 2023 |
| **INDEP/KAS** (citing AMMK) | Total municipal waste **collected** | 471,278 t (2023), 449,708 t (2024) | 2023-2024 |

**Reading:** These measure different things — what's *produced* vs. what the formal system *captures*. 490k generated minus ~471k collected = ~19k tonnes uncollected (illegal dumping + informal channels + losses). Roughly consistent at ~96% formal capture rate. Use both: the dossier's "generation" figure and INDEP/KAS's "collected" series complement each other. (Reframed from a prior misclassification of this as a number-tension.)

### Tension X: Strategy date — 2024-2030 vs 2024-2035

| Source | Strategy framing | Date range |
|---|---|---|
| INDEP/KAS extraction (`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md` §5) | "Strategy and Action Plan for Integrated Waste Management 2024-2030" (revised April 2024) | 2024-2030 |
| **Mazreku/MMPHI deck** (`enrichment-summary-mazreku-newlaw.md` §3) | "Integrated Waste Management Strategy 2024-2035, approved by the Government in December 2024" | 2024-2035 |

**Reading:** Possible reconciliations include: (a) 2024-2030 = the Action Plan period vs 2024-2035 = the full Strategy period (action plans are typically shorter than the strategies they implement); (b) two separate approval events (April 2024 revision + December 2024 government approval); (c) a revision that extended the end date. Worth verifying against the official MMPHI document before citing either date as canonical.

### Tension Y: DRS readiness gap

| Source | DRS legal status | Implementation timing |
|---|---|---|
| INDEP/KAS extraction | "Sistemi i Rimbursimit të Depozitave (SRD/DRS) — secondary legislation passed 2024" | "planned implementation January 2025" |
| **Mazreku/MMPHI deck** (`enrichment-summary-mazreku-newlaw.md` §6) | DRS legal base established by the **NEW** Law on Integrated Waste Management | Government approval Feb-Mar 2026; secondary legislation Feb-Mar 2027 |

**Reading:** The 2024 secondary legislation appears to have been incomplete or insufficient — the new Law replaces or strengthens it, with its own secondary-legislation cycle starting in 2027. This makes follow-up #3 (verify whether DRS actually launched January 2025) considerably more important: the most likely scenario is that the planned January 2025 launch slipped or launched in a partial form, which is part of why the new Law was needed. Find out what actually happened on the ground in early 2025.

### Tension Z: Methodology shift in waste-generation figures

| Source | Figure | Year |
|---|---|---|
| DYVÓ extraction (`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md` §4) | 247,000 t total Kosovo waste generation | 2014 |
| DYVÓ extraction | 284,000 t total Kosovo waste generation | 2018 |
| INDEP/KAS extraction | 471,278 t **collected** | 2023 |
| INDEP/KAS extraction | 449,708 t **collected** | 2024 |

**Reading:** The 2018 → 2023 jump (+66% in five years) is implausibly large as a pure generation increase. Possible drivers, in likely combination:

- **(a) Genuine growth from MPG-CE coverage expansion (2017-2021):** GIZ data shows coverage went from 70.9% to 90.2% in that window — formalising ~20 points of previously-uncaptured waste alone explains roughly 30% of the apparent jump.
- **(b) Methodology shift between AMMK reports:** the older (DYVÓ-cited) figures may have been *generation* estimates while INDEP/KAS reports *collected* tonnage; mixing the two without flagging it inflates the trend.
- **(c) Population and consumption growth** during the period.

Most likely a real-but-amplified rise: actual generation did climb, but the headline +66% is partly an artefact of better measurement and broader collection coverage. Don't use the 2014/2018 → 2023/2024 figures as a single trend line without footnoting the methodology gap.

## ➕ Major additions to the picture

The original dossier was light on **policy stack** and **measurable outcomes**. INDEP/KAS adds:

### Policy stack now in force or imminent

- **Strategy and Action Plan for Integrated Waste Management 2024-2030** (revised April 2024) — see Tension X above for end-date discrepancy
- **Roadmap for Circular Economy** (MMPHI, 2023)
- **Action Plan 2024-2026** with **€41 million** committed
- **Total CE investments planned through 2030: €77 million**
- **Administrative Order 04/2025** — plastic bag tariffs, took effect 2025
- **Deposit Refund System (SRD/DRS)** — secondary legislation passed 2024, **rollout planned January 2025** (need to verify if launched on time — see Tension Y)

### One measurable policy win

- **Plastic bag imports dropped 68.4%** between 2023 and 2024 after the Administrative Order. The system is movable. This is a load-bearing data point for any reform argument.

### Industrial waste streams the dossier underweighted

- **Construction & demolition waste:** ~863,000 tonnes generated 2016-2020. The dossier mentioned ~167,900 t/year from MMPHI — likely a different measurement window. INDEP/KAS confirms C&D is the largest single stream.
- **Thermal power plant ash:** >1 million tonnes/year (dossier missed this entirely)
- **Trepça mining complex accumulated waste:** ~60 million tonnes (dossier missed this entirely)

### Resource productivity gap (a strong economic argument)

- Western Balkans: **€0.35/kg** of material consumed
- EU average: **€2.07/kg**
- **6× gap** — reframes the circular economy from "environmental concern" to "economic catch-up." Worth using when pitching the system to decision-makers.

### EU policy context

The dossier mentioned EU accession pressure but didn't catalogue the policy stack. INDEP/KAS adds:
- EU CEAP 2020, ESPR 2024, Digital Product Passport, EU Battery Regulation, Packaging & Packaging Waste Regulation, EU Sustainable Textiles Strategy, Circular Electronics Initiative, Acquis Chapter 27, Green Agenda for Western Balkans 2020 (Kosovo committed), EU Reform & Growth Facility 2024-2027 (€6 billion)
