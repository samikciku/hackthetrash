## 3. Six priority value chains (the pyramid)

The document identifies six priority chains, ranked by signal value. From the pyramid figure (p.26):

| Rank | Chain | Why it's prioritized | Lead policy instrument |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Packaging & Plastics** | High visibility, large municipal waste share, strong policy levers (DRS, EPR) | **Deposit Refund System (SRD/DRS)** launching Jan 2025 |
| **2** | Bio-waste | Major share of municipal waste; composting + biogas potential | Mandatory source separation; composting infrastructure |
| **3** | Construction & Demolition Waste | Largest volume stream; high recovery potential (concrete, steel, aggregates) | **80% recycling target** in policy; selective demolition |
| **4** | WEEE + Batteries | High material value (Cu, Al, rare metals) **AND** high environmental risk (Pb, Hg, Cd) | EPR systems for electronics + batteries |
| **5** | Textiles + Consumer Goods | Employment + social entrepreneurship potential; existing reuse markets | Reuse/repair centers; second-hand market formalization |
| **6** | Industrial + Mining Waste | Largest volume by tonnage; highest environmental risk | Industrial symbiosis platform; MINT regulation |

Per the pyramid in the doc, packaging/plastic is prioritized for first action despite being mid-rank in the pyramid — because it has the strongest existing policy lever (the planned 2025 DRS).

## 6. Document's diagnosis of the system

### What's working (or starting to)

- ✅ Plastic bag imports down 68.4% post-Administrative Order 04/2025 ⚠ *(see internal-inconsistency note below)*
- ✅ Composting plant in Pristina handling green/garden waste
- ✅ Pilot composting program in Vushtrri municipality
- ✅ Home composting programs (donor-supported)
- ✅ **Ujë Miros** (private company) — glass bottle reuse system, demonstrated economic viability
- ✅ Trepça lead battery recycling — industrial-scale; Kosovo has actual recycling capacity for one specific stream
- ✅ Strategic framework approved (Strategy + Roadmap + Action Plan)
- ✅ DRS legislation passed (implementation pending)

> ⚠ **Internal inconsistency in the source document.** The doc attributes the 68.4% plastic-bag-imports drop (measured 2023→2024 per AMMK) to Administrative Order **04/2025**. A 2025-numbered order can't have caused a 2023-2024 drop. Possible reconciliations: (a) Order 04/2025 codifies a 2023 or 2024 precursor with a different number; (b) the import drop was driven by import-tariff changes that pre-date the named order. Verify against the underlying admin order text before citing this as proof the system responds to policy.

### What's not working

- ❌ Recycling rate stuck at <3% (vs EU 44%)
- ❌ ~90%+ of municipal waste still landfilled
- ❌ Bio-waste: only 5% separated/treated
- ❌ Bio-waste decomposing in landfills generates methane (climate burden)
- ❌ Construction & demolition waste mixes with municipal stream → undermines recovery
- ❌ Compliance with planning requirements: 65% (down from 66%)
- ❌ Service coverage dropped 93.6% → 90.9% (rural gaps growing)
- ❌ Illegal dumpsites grew 403 → 458 in one year
- ❌ **Inspection systems underfunded, understaffed** — explicit document finding
- ❌ Public participation in source separation: limited
- ❌ Public awareness: limited; CE not in school curricula
- ❌ Per capita waste generation **rising** as incomes rise (240 → 294 kg)

### Structural gaps the document explicitly names

1. **No mandatory operational targets** for municipalities or operators (only national-level aspirations)
2. **No performance-based funding** for municipal waste services
3. **Fragmented data systems** — no national material-flow database
4. **Weak inter-ministerial coordination** between MMPHI, MINT, Ministry of Agriculture
5. **Insufficient inspection capacity** — directly enables illegal dumping
6. **Underdeveloped secondary materials markets**
7. **Limited financing mechanisms for circular SMEs**

## 7. The 15 Policy Recommendations (Chapter 8 verbatim)

The document closes with a structured implementation matrix. Each row = one recommendation with legal step, lead institution, and risk mitigation. Reproduced as a table:

| # | Recommendation | Legal/regulatory step | Lead institution | Risk mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **National data system for circular economy + material flows** | Amend Waste Law; pass secondary acts requiring material-flow + treatment reporting | MMPHI, AMMK, KAS (Statistics) | Build digital reporting platform; provide technical assistance to reporting entities |
| 2 | **Inter-Ministerial Circular Economy Coordination Unit** | Govt decision establishing permanent inter-ministerial body | MMPHI, Office of the Prime Minister | Define mandate, staffing, reporting responsibilities clearly |
| 3 | **EPR systems for priority waste streams** | Pass secondary acts operationalizing EPR for packaging, WEEE, batteries, textiles | MMPHI, MINT | Phased rollout starting with packaging; licensing + enforcement mechanisms |
| 4 | **DRS national rollout** | Pass implementing regulations for system governance + deposit levels | MMPHI, MINT | Pilot in main municipalities before national rollout |
| 5 | **Performance-based municipal financing** | Amend municipal financing rules to tie transfers to recycling + landfill-diversion performance | MMPHI, Ministry of Finance, municipalities | Financial + technical support to municipalities |
| 6 | **Mandatory source separation for recyclables + bio-waste** | Pass regulation requiring separation at source | MMPHI, municipalities | Phased implementation with infrastructure support + awareness campaigns |
| 7 | **National investment program: recycling, composting, sorting infrastructure** | Adopt national investment program for CE infrastructure | MMPHI, Ministry of Finance, municipalities, donors | Mobilize donor + PPP financing |
| 8 | **C&D waste recovery system** | Pass regulations requiring sorting + recycling targets for construction/demolition | MMPHI, municipalities | Contractor compliance requirements + enforcement mechanisms |
| 9 | **Industrial symbiosis platform** | Develop digital platform + regulatory framework for industrial materials exchange | MMPHI, MINT | Financial incentives + industry engagement programs |
| 10 | **Landfill tax to incentivize recycling** | Amend waste law to introduce landfill disposal tax | MMPHI, Ministry of Finance | Gradual rate increase to avoid incentivizing illegal dumping |
| 11 | **Reuse + repair centers in main municipalities** | Adopt municipal + national programs for reuse centers | MMPHI, municipalities | Public-private partnerships + donor support |
| 12 | **Green public procurement requirements** | Amend public procurement law to include CE criteria | Ministry of Finance, Public Procurement Regulatory Commission | Procurement guidelines + training |
| 13 | **Strengthen enforcement against illegal dumping** | Increase inspection capacity + enforcement of sanctions | MMPHI, municipalities, inspectorates | More inspector staff + digital reporting tools |
| 14 | **Financial schemes for CE innovation** | Establish fund / financing window for CE innovation | Ministry of Finance, MINT, donors | Combined financing + grant support |
| 15 | **National education + awareness program for CE** | Integrate CE into education system + public campaigns | MMPHI, Ministry of Education | Public campaigns + school programs |

## 8. How this connects to the existing dossier

### Confirms (✓) the dossier's claims

- ✓ Illegal dumpsite count of 458 (2024) — matches dossier exactly
- ✓ Western Balkans recycling rate <3%
- ✓ Most waste landfilled, no national sorting plant
- ✓ Construction/demolition waste mixing with municipal stream
- ✓ Informal recycling (RAE scavengers) doing the recycling work the system formally claims
- ✓ KLMC operates landfills under Ministry of Economy oversight
- ✓ MMPHI is the lead waste-policy authority

### Extends (➕) the dossier

- ➕ **Adds the policy stack the dossier was missing.** Strategy 2024-2030, Roadmap for CE, Administrative Order 04/2025, DRS legislation, Action Plan 2024-2026.
- ➕ **Confirms 2024 numbers.** Per-capita waste 294 kg, total 449,708 tonnes collected. The dossier had 490,000 tonnes for 2023; this is an updated, slightly different figure (different methodology/year).
- ➕ **Plastic bag imports down 68.4%** — adds a measurable policy outcome the dossier didn't have.
- ➕ **The €77M circular-economy investment commitment** + the €41M action plan — these are concrete budget lines.
- ➕ **The 6-tier priority pyramid** is a clean framing the dossier lacked.
- ➕ **Resource productivity gap** (€0.35/kg vs EU €2.07/kg) — quantifies the structural economic argument.
- ➕ **Industrial waste volumes** (1M+ t/yr power plant ash; 60M t Trepça mining waste) — these are massive streams the dossier underweighted.
- ➕ **Trepça's lead battery recycling capability** — Kosovo has working recycling for *one* stream, useful for the simulation as a working example.

### Tensions (⚠) with the dossier

- ⚠ **Service coverage figure.** Dossier said 50-85% (depending on methodology). Document says 93.6% (2023) → 90.9% (2024). The document figure is **much higher** and from AMMK directly. Reconciliation: dossier was citing different metric (formal vs informal coverage, urban vs rural). Worth flagging in the dossier that there are at least two different "coverage" numbers in circulation.
- ⚠ **Total municipal waste figure.** Dossier: ~490,000 t/yr (2023). Document: 471,278 t (2023), 449,708 t (2024). Document is more authoritative + recent.
- ⚠ The document **does not address** the Pastrimi-Komuna registry dispute, Pastrimi-KLMC debt situation, or the political-party alignment issues that the dossier flagged. INDEP's framing is national-policy-level, not Pristina-operations-level. The two documents are complementary, not competing.

### What this document doesn't cover (dossier still authoritative)

- The Pastrimi vs Komuna registry dispute (95k vs 72k clients) — invisible here
- The €1.9M Pastrimi → KLMC debt situation
- Worker strikes / union politics (Ardian Krasniqi, May-June 2025)
- Mirash landfill specifics (methane risks, the "landfill lake")
- RAE / Roma scavenger community as a labor force
- Kosovo's ranking as 3rd worst globally for unsorted household waste

## 9. Implications for the hackathon's two project tracks

### For the Trash Reporter app (HackTheTrash)

- **EPR + DRS context** means the app's "route to authority" feature has real upcoming legal underpinning. Plastic bottles report → DRS infrastructure (when live). E-waste report → EPR system for WEEE. Worth designing the routing layer to anticipate these channels.
- **Inspection capacity is the #1 enforcement bottleneck** named in the document. The app's report → authority pipeline is filling exactly the gap that document flags as preventing illegal-dumping enforcement.
- **The document explicitly calls for "digital reporting tools"** for inspectors (Recommendation 13). This app could be exactly that tool.

### For the Trash Management Sim

- The 15 recommendations make excellent **player-action cards**. Each is a "policy lever" the player can pull, with an associated cost (legal step), authority (lead institution), and consequence (risk mitigation).
- The 6-tier priority pyramid is a natural **scoring axis**. Player optimizes which streams they tackle first.
- The €0.35/kg vs €2.07/kg gap = the **end-state win condition** ("close the productivity gap to EU level").
- Trepça lead battery recycling = the player's **starting working node**.
- Inspection underfunding = the structural drag the player must overcome.
