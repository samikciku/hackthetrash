## 2. The headline framework: implementation is a chain, not a status

The doc opens with the core analytical move: **a law/project decision is NOT implementation**. A law creates the legal basis. Implementation requires secondary legislation, budget allocation, permits, procurement, contracts, execution, payments, supervision, and finally operational use.

> *"Miratimi i ligjit nuk nënkupton zbatim automatik të projektit"* — passing the law does not mean automatic implementation.

### The 10-stage implementation chain (verbatim from the doc)

| # | Stage | Albanian term | What it means | Is it implemented? |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Idea | Ideja e projektit / nevoja publike | Policy need identified | No |
| 2 | Draft law/policy | Projektligj / politikë publike | Legal/policy proposal drafted | No |
| 3 | Government approval | Vendim i Qeverisë / miratim nga Qeveria | Proposal sent forward | No |
| 4 | Assembly process | Shqyrtim në Kuvend / amendamente / miratim | Law adopted | Still not enough |
| 5 | Entry into force | Publikim në Gazetën Zyrtare / hyrje në fuqi | Legal basis becomes enforceable | Legal basis only |
| 6 | Secondary acts | Akte nënligjore / udhëzime administrative / rregullore | Implementation rules issued | Partially ready |
| 7 | Budget | Ndarje buxhetore / alokim buxhetor | Money assigned | Enables implementation |
| 8 | Permits | Pëlqim mjedisor / leje ndërtimore / leje mjedisore | Required approvals obtained | Enables physical work |
| 9 | Procurement | Tender / prokurim publik / kontratë publike | Contractor/operator selected | Implementation may begin |
| 10a | Execution | Zbatim i kontratës / punime / shërbime / pagesa | Work/service delivery | In progress |
| 10b | Operation | Funksionalizim / vënie në funksion / operim | Service/facility actually works | Implemented |

**Why this is the load-bearing finding:** every stage where the chain breaks is a different kind of system failure. The 2024-25 Pastrimi-Komuna crisis is fundamentally a **stage 9 / 10a problem** (procurement + execution disputes, not absence of legal basis). The new Law on Integrated Waste Management (per Mazreku) is between **stages 4 and 5** (Assembly approval expected Aug-Sep 2026). The DRS (per the dossier) is between **stages 5 and 6** (entry into force passed; secondary acts pending).

This single framework gives every other piece of dossier work a **stage-of-implementation tag**. Useful for the Sim and for any policy critique.

## 3. Public enterprises: the Sh.A. confusion clarified

A common misreading the doc explicitly addresses: **`Sh.A.` does NOT mean private company.**

- **Sh.A. = Shoqëri Aksionare = joint-stock company** — a corporate form
- Public enterprises in Kosovo are organized AS joint-stock companies (Sh.A.) under business/company law, but with shares held by the state or municipalities
- An enterprise is treated as public when **50%+ ownership** is held by Republic of Kosovo and/or one or more public authorities. If ownership drops below 50%, it loses public-enterprise status.

### Two flavors of public enterprise

| Type | Albanian | Owner / shareholder |
|---|---|---|
| **Central public enterprise** (NP Qendrore) | Ndërmarrje Publike Qendrore | Republic of Kosovo |
| **Local public enterprise** (NP Lokale) | Ndërmarrje Publike Lokale | One or more municipalities |

### Concrete classifications

- **KMDK / KLMC** = **Central** public enterprise. State-owned landfill/disposal company. Reports up through the Government → responsible Ministry → Board → CEO → operations chain.
- **Pastrimi (KRM Pastrimi Sh.A.)** = **Local** public enterprise. Owned through municipal public-enterprise framework. Reports up through Municipal shareholders → Municipal Shareholders' Commission → Board → CEO → operations.

### The local-enterprise board (5 directors)

A local public enterprise board has **5 directors**:
- 4 elected by shareholders (the municipalities)
- 1 is the CEO selected by the board itself

If more than one municipality owns shares, **each municipality's Shareholders' Commission has rights in the director-selection process**.

### The Municipal Shareholders' Commission (Komision Komunal i Aksionarëve)

A 3-member body that exercises shareholder rights for the municipality:
- 1 member appointed by the **mayor**
- 2 members appointed by the **municipal assembly**

This is what a municipality formally uses to "own" Pastrimi. **Mayor and Assembly do not directly run the company** — they vote through this commission.

### One-line summary

> "Sh.A. describes the corporate form. Ownership depends on who holds the shares. A Sh.A. can be public if the shares are held by the state or municipalities."

## 4. KMDK / KLMC: where it sits in the hierarchy

**KMDK = Kompania për Menaxhimin e Deponive në Kosovë Sh.A.**
English: **Kosovo Landfill Management Company / KLMC**

> "KMDK is the state-owned landfill/disposal company."

Governance chain:
**Republic of Kosovo / Government → Ministry/public-enterprise monitoring framework → Board of Directors → CEO → landfill/disposal operations**

Operationally, **KMDK manages disposal sites; waste collection companies bring waste to those sites.** This separation is structural — when household collection fails, that's not KMDK. When landfill capacity, tariffs, debts, or access fails, that becomes KMDK.

---

## 5. Pastrimi: where it sits in the hierarchy

**KRM "Pastrimi" Sh.A. = Kompania Rajonale e Mbeturinave "Pastrimi" Sh.A.**

> "Pastrimi is also not a private company. It is a local/regional publicly owned enterprise."

Governance chain (different from KMDK's):
**Municipal shareholder(s) → Municipal Shareholders' Commission(s) → Board of Directors → CEO / Management → collection and transport operations**

| Company | Main function |
|---|---|
| Pastrimi | Collection and transport of municipal waste |
| KMDK | Landfill/disposal-site management |

The Waste Law backs this division: municipalities organize collection through special contracts with licensed operators; Pastrimi is the licensed operator for its territory.

---

## 6. Municipality role — six listed responsibilities

Under the Waste Law, municipalities are NOT passive. They:
1. Create the municipal waste-management system
2. Draft local waste-management plans
3. Make sure municipal plans match the national plan
4. Organize and regulate service delivery
5. Select licensed persons/operators through procurement
6. Set tariffs and fee-collection methods
7. Supervise and control waste-management measures through municipal environmental inspectors

**Structural tension:** Where the municipality owns shares in a company like Pastrimi, it is **simultaneously a service organizer/regulator AND a shareholder**. The municipality is not the same as Pastrimi, but it owns and supervises Pastrimi via shareholder mechanisms.

## 7. Where the blockages appear (six diagnostic patterns)

The doc names six specific blockage patterns — each one points at a different stage in the implementation chain. **These are essentially six distinct failure modes for the Sim to model.**

### A. Legal basis exists, but implementation is fragmented
> "Laws and strategies exist, but the implementation chain breaks at budget, enforcement, procurement, contracts, tariffs, data, or institutional coordination."

### B. Central-local split
Central government handles policy / legal framework / licensing / strategy / central public enterprises like KMDK. Municipalities handle local waste plans / collection organization / tariffs / contracts / local enforcement. Legal split is normal; in practice it creates gaps when one level depends on the other.

### C. KMDK-Pastrimi split (the cash-flow chain)
Pastrimi depends on waste-fee revenue to pay workers, fuel, trucks, disposal costs. KMDK depends on operators like Pastrimi paying landfill/disposal fees. **If citizens don't pay, or if municipality/operator dispute who collects the fee, cash-flow problems move through the entire chain.**

The 2025 Prishtina/Pastrimi dispute illustrates this exactly. Pastrimi is a separate public company with its own payroll, fuel costs, landfill fees, debts, and bank accounts. The dispute was about who controls/receives waste-fee money in Prishtina. **KALLXO reported** that the Supreme Court refused Prishtina Municipality's request to suspend the amended waste regulation, and that the regulation provided for waste bills to be paid directly to KRM "Pastrimi" Sh.A. **The crisis began on 13 May 2025 when Pastrimi stopped collection, citing a €2.7 million municipal debt.**

### D. Municipal ownership ≠ municipal department
> "Even if a municipality owns shares in Pastrimi, Pastrimi is still a separate company. It has a board, CEO, budget, workers, liabilities, and contracts. The municipality acts as shareholder and service organizer, but the company performs operations."

A blockage appears when the municipality acts politically/administratively while the company needs predictable operational revenue. This is the structural heart of the 2024-25 crisis.

### E. Tariff and billing uncertainty
The Waste Law gives municipalities authority over tariffs and collection methods. But when the operator provides the service AND needs direct cash flow, disputes over whether citizens pay the municipality or the operator can destabilize the whole service.

> "The system may be public on all sides, but still fail because the money flow is unclear or contested."

### F. Weak enforcement, weak data, incomplete licensing/permitting
The national strategy itself identifies the need to strengthen regulation and control, create a comprehensive waste information system, and expand licensing/permits. Waste collection coverage has improved but is still not universal.

> "The blockage is not only 'who collects trash,' but also: weak data, incomplete licensing, weak inspection, weak enforcement, and lack of full service coverage."

## 8. Simple responsibility map (the doc's clean summary)

| Level | Institution / company | Responsibility |
|---|---|---|
| **Central policy** | MMPHI / environmental ministry framework | Waste law, strategy, environmental rules, licensing framework, national reporting |
| **Central public-enterprise ownership** | Government / Ministry of Economy / monitoring unit | Shareholder supervision of central public enterprises |
| **Disposal infrastructure** | KMDK / KLMC Sh.A. | Landfill and disposal-site management |
| **Local government** | Municipality / Komuna | Local waste plan, tariffs, collection rules, contracting, local inspection, public-space waste |
| **Local public enterprise** | KRM Pastrimi Sh.A. | Collection and transport in its service area |
| **Users** | Households / businesses | Use service, separate/handle waste where required, pay fees |

This 6-tier table is the cleanest one-shot summary of Kosovo's waste-management institutional architecture from any source we have.

## 9. The doc's main conclusion

> "Kosovo's weak waste-management system is not only a technical issue. It is an institutional-chain issue."

The strongest argument it offers:

> "Kosovo's waste system fails where legal responsibility, corporate governance, municipal service delivery, and cash flow do not align. Laws and public ownership exist, but implementation depends on contracts, payment, enforcement, municipal capacity, and functioning coordination between central and local institutions."

This frames the system's problem as **alignment failure**, not law absence. That's a meaningfully different diagnosis from "Kosovo doesn't have the right laws" — and it leads to different interventions.

## 12. Implications for the hackathon's two project tracks

### For HackTheTrash (citizen reporting)
- The implementation chain explains why citizen reports are valuable: the gap between "Stage 4 — Law adopted" and "Stage 10 — Operation" is precisely where citizen evidence becomes load-bearing. - **The 10-stage implementation chain IS a Sim mechanic.** Players can advance a card from one stage to the next; cards stuck at certain stages create different system-effect signatures.
- **The 6 blockage patterns (A-F)** are six categorical failure modes. Each Sim turn could roll for one of these.
- **The 5-director board + Shareholders' Commission** structure adds detail to the trash-map's actor model. Could be encoded as sub-nodes inside Pastrimi's actor entry.

## 14. One-line takeaway

> Kosovo's waste system has the laws. What it lacks — and what every blockage pattern points at — is **alignment between legal responsibility, corporate governance, municipal service delivery, and cash flow.** The system fails at the seams between those four, not at the absence of any one.
