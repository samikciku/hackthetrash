## 2. The MPG-CE program (2020-2022) — what was tried and what worked

**Municipal Performance Grant – Clean Environment**, owned by Kosovo Government, led by **MESPI** (= MMPHI, Ministry of Environment, Spatial Planning and Infrastructure — see acronym note below).

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Implementation period | 2020-2022 (3 years) |
| Total invested | **€7 million** (Kosovo Gov + EU + German Gov) |
| Target municipalities | 38 (all of Kosovo) |
| Awarded municipalities | **24 of 38** (~63% participation rate) |
| Mechanism | Performance-based — access criteria + indicators in line with KIWMS + EU accession requirements |

### Equipment / infrastructure delivered

- 23 collection trucks
- 4 skipper trucks
- 11 sweeping machines
- 3 tractors
- 3,000 × 240L containers
- 11,000 × 120L containers
- 2,400 × 1.1m³ containers
- 3,500 home composters

That's **16,400 containers** plus a fleet expansion. For context: the dossier's Pastrimi-only fleet count (Pristina) is 14 + 3 + 10 = 27 trucks.

## 3. Time series — the headline finding

The slides show a multi-year progress arc that reconciles the dossier-vs-INDEP/KAS coverage tension:

| Indicator | 2016 | 2017 | 2021 | 2022 | 2023 | 2024 |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Waste service coverage | **54%** | 70.9% | **90.2%** | — | 93.6% | 90.9% |
| Illegal landfills (count) | — | **2,246** | — | **763** | 403 | 458 |
| Fee collection rate | — | 80.64% | 81.80% | — | 89% | 90.4% |
| Municipalities reporting to KEPA/AMMK | — | **3** | **35** | — | — | — |

**This is the picture:** Kosovo's waste system jumped from 54% coverage and 2,246 illegal landfills in 2016-17 to 90.2% coverage and 763 landfills in 2021-22 — substantially because of the MPG-CE scheme. The post-2022 numbers (from AMMK via INDEP/KAS) show some regression in coverage and a small uptick in illegal sites (403 → 458), suggesting **gains held but momentum stalled when MPG-CE ended**.

### Other 2017→2021 progress shown

- 70,000+ new households added to formal collection
- 30,162 households in 17 municipalities now have access to **separate collection** (source separation infrastructure)
- Municipalities reporting annually to KEPA went from **3 to 35** out of 38

### Reading

This is hard evidence that **performance-based municipal funding works in Kosovo's waste sector** — exactly what INDEP/KAS Recommendation 5 calls for. The doc is asking for a bigger version because the smaller version delivered measurable returns.

## 4. The proposed CE-PG (Circular Economy Performance Grant)

GIZ's proposed successor to MPG-CE.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Implementation period | **2024-2027** |
| Funds requested | **€20 million** |
| Total KIWMS implementation budget | €105.15 million (i.e., the €20M ask is ~19% of the full strategy) |
| Lead institution | MESPI (= MMPHI) |
| Funding sources | MESPI as scheme owner, EU, German Government, other donors, other line ministries (MRD, MLGA, MoE, MoA), municipal co-financing |

### What CE-PG would fund (per slide 3)

A list of waste/CE infrastructure types:
- **CACs** — Civic Amenity Centers
- **TSs** — Transfer Stations
- **MRFs** — Materials Recovery Facilities (sorting plants)
- **IMC** — Inter-Municipal Cooperation
- **C&DWs** — Construction & Demolition Waste facilities
- **Composting Plants**
- **Landfills**

This is the physical infrastructure layer Kosovo doesn't have. **No national waste sorting plant exists** (per dossier); MRFs would be the first.

### CE-PG access criteria (4-tier funnel)

The slides describe a layered access structure:
1. **Bound to IMC** — must participate in inter-municipal cooperation
2. **Compliance** — legal + institutional alignment with EU acquis
3. **Basic Services** — sustainable service provision, "0 illegal dumpsites" policy, cost efficiency
4. **Circular Economy** — separate collection, specific waste-fraction systems

Each tier is a higher gate. Municipalities must clear lower tiers to access higher-tier funding. **This is a working policy-architecture pattern** — incentives stacked progressively.

### Three procurement options proposed (decision pending)

The slides explicitly present three options with trade-offs — this is **decision-making material, not policy declaration**:

| Option | Mechanism | Advantage | Disadvantage |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Municipalities receive funds separately through SWMP | Municipalities decide themselves | Doesn't contribute to inter-municipal coordination |
| 2 | Municipalities **jointly** invest toward IMC / IIWMP implementation | Larger investments, supports IIWMP | Less incentive for smaller municipalities |
| 3 | MESPI allocates funds + procures **on behalf** of municipalities | MESPI has the Action Plan in place | Procurement workload may overload MESPI capacity |

No option declared chosen. **The Sim could model this directly as a player choice node.**

### CE-PG implementation 7-step process

1. ECE-PG/F launched (2024)
2. MoUs signed — municipalities invited to participate
3. Municipalities report (KEPA/AMMK as main data source)
4. Municipalities evaluated against access criteria + performance indicators
5. Municipalities awarded
6. Winning grants (formula agreed by Steering Committee)
7. Investments in place

A **Steering Committee** is named as the main decision-making body throughout.

## 7. Acronym disambiguation — MESPI vs MMPHI

This deck uses **MESPI** for the same ministry that INDEP/KAS calls **MMPHI**. They are the same institution:

- **MESPI** = Ministry of Environment, Spatial Planning and Infrastructure (English form, used by GIZ + EU partners)
- **MMPHI** = Ministria e Mjedisit, Planifikimit Hapësinor dhe Infrastrukturës (Albanian form, used in Albanian-language documents)
- **MESP** = Ministry of Environment and Spatial Planning (older English form, before "Infrastructure" was added)
- The original dossier (2025) uses **MMPHI**; this deck (2026) uses **MESPI**

Suggest standardizing on **MMPHI** in dossier (per Albanian-language source) but noting MESPI as English equivalent in `acronyms.md`.
