## 2. The 14 survey questions (with response options)

The instrument had 20 questions; pandoc preserved 14 cleanly. Order from the doc:

### Q1 — Frequency of seeing waste outside containers
- Never / Rarely / Sometimes / Often / Always

### Q2 — Most polluted areas (multi-select)
- Rivers, parks, forests, residential, abandoned, commercial, industrial, suburbs, villages, areas around workshops/factories, **Peja train station**, all, almost all of the above

### Q3 — Have you been fined? (multi-select)
- Yes (personal), no, know a business that was, know someone who was

### Q4 — Biggest waste challenges (multi-select)
- Poor management, lack of recycling bins, inadequate container size, too few containers, lack of recycling containers, irregular collection, inadequate maintenance

### Q5 — Government engagement on environmental law
- Very engaged / Sufficiently / Slightly / Not at all

### Q6 — Main cause of illegal waste (up to 2)
- Lack of infrastructure, insufficient enforcement, lack of public awareness, individual unwillingness, citizens produce too much, businesses/cafés responsible, "other reasons"

### Q7 — Why government isn't doing enough (open-ended responses)

Selected response options preserved:
- "Has not issued legal instruments"

- "Has invested very little"
- "Has not punished environmental polluters"
- "**NO POLITICAL INTEREST**" (in caps in source)
- "Lacks administrative staff"

### Q8 — Have you reported illegal waste dumping?
- Yes / No (the DYVÓ doc reports almost all said No)

### Q9 — Recycling behavior
- Yes, recycle and reuse / Want to but don't know how / Don't know where to take waste / No, don't recycle

### Q10 — What counts as "illegal waste"? (multi-select)
- Plastic, hazardous/medical, electronic, food, construction, outside containers, business waste, furniture, all, animal waste, inappropriate-place waste, bulky waste, construction products, chemical waste

### Q11 — Knowledge of where to report illegal waste (4 options)
1. Yes, know what + know institution
2. Yes, know what but not institution
3. No, don't know what but know institution

### Q12 — Plastic perception
- Not harmful / Unavoidable / Harmful

### Q13 — Microplastics awareness
- Yes / No (the DYVÓ doc reports most said No)

### Q14 — Frequency of receiving environmental-law information
- Never / Rarely / Sometimes / Often / Very often

### Q15 — Plastic waste → serious health problems?
- Yes / No / I don't know

## 3. New findings from the question set (beyond the main DYVÓ summary)

Most question content was already inferable from the main DYVÓ summary. Two genuinely new items:

1. **"Need for a Green Police unit"** is a citizen-proposed institution. The survey treated it as a real response option, suggesting it surfaced repeatedly in focus groups before being coded. *Could become a Sim card or a real policy proposal.*

2. **"Peja train station"** specifically named as a polluted area in the multi-select. Weirdly specific; suggests Peja focus-group respondents flagged it strongly enough to make the option list. Worth noting if anyone is researching Peja.

## 4. Survey methodology gaps

The survey instrument has some methodology issues worth flagging:

- **"All" / "Almost all of the above"** options on Q2 (polluted areas) — undermines multi-select cleanliness; inflates affirmative responses.
- **Q4 has duplicate-feeling options** ("lack of recycling containers/bins" + "lack of recycling containers") — possibly translation artifact, possibly genuine duplication that would inflate that category.
- **Q7 mixes free-text and pre-coded options** — quantitative vs qualitative analysis approach unclear.
- **n=150 with snowball sampling via social media** is small + biased toward the digitally engaged. The DYVÓ doc notes 21-25-year-olds were the largest cohort.

These don't invalidate findings; they just calibrate confidence. Citizen perceptions trend similar to focus-group qualitative findings, so the survey is consistent with itself.
