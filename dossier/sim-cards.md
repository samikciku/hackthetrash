# Sim Cards — 21 Policy Levers for the Pristina Trash System

> A card deck of policy interventions for the Trash, Please simulation. Each card is one recommendation that's been formally proposed for Kosovo's waste system. Cards have a lead institution (who's accountable), key supporting actors (who does the work), and dependency relationships with other cards. Play order at the bottom.
>
> Cards 1-15 come from the **INDEP/KAS April 2026 policy document** (`enrichment-summary-pdf2.md` §7). Cards 16-21 come from **NGO DYVÓ's 2023 plastic + commercial-waste research** (`enrichment-summary-dyvo-plastic.md` §7) — complementary, not duplicate.
>
> ~80% confident on cards 1-15 (INDEP/KAS named lead institutions explicitly). ~75% on cards 16-21 (DYVÓ named the recommendation; supporting roles inferred from context).

## Actor codes

Full names in [`acronyms.md`](acronyms.md). For Sim purposes:

- **MMPHI** — Ministry of Environment, Spatial Planning, Infrastructure (load-bearing institution)
- **AMMK** — Kosovo EPA, under MMPHI
- **MINT** — Ministry of Industry, Entrepreneurship, Trade
- **MoFin** — Ministry of Finance
- **MoEd** — Ministry of Education
- **CG** — Central Government / Office of the Prime Minister
- **KOM** — Komuna (municipalities)
- **INSP** — municipal/environmental inspectorate
- **EU** — EU Office in Pristina + IPA + RGF
- **DON** — donor coordination (GIZ, JICA, World Bank)
- **PRO** — Producer Responsibility Organizations (don't yet exist)
- **IND/CON** — industrial + construction sector
- **COMM** — commercial-sector businesses
- **NGO** — civil society
- **PRESS** — media
- **CIT** — citizens / households

## INDEP/KAS cards (1-15)

### Card 1 — National data system for material flows
- **Lead:** MMPHI (policy A) + AMMK (operational R)
- **Key actors:** KOM, IND/CON (mandatory reporters)
- **Status:** Foundational. Currently fragmented data systems per INDEP/KAS itself.
- **Unlocks:** Card 5 (can't reward outcomes without data); also enables Card 18 (commercial PAYT).

### Card 2 — Inter-Ministerial CE Coordination Unit
- **Lead:** MMPHI (policy A) + CG (establishment R via government decision)
- **Key actors:** all line ministries consulted
- **Status:** Doesn't exist.
- **Unlocks:** Cards 3, 6, 7, 8 — anything cross-ministerial. **High-leverage card.**

### Card 3 — EPR for priority streams (packaging, WEEE, batteries, textiles)
- **Lead:** MMPHI; PROs operationally R (but PROs don't exist yet — "R to-form")
- **Key actors:** MINT (industrial implementation), IND/CON (producers, consulted)
- **Status:** Strong legal base coming in new Law on Integrated WM. Operating PROs unverified.
- **Unlocks:** Card 4 (DRS as specific EPR implementation), Card 19 (business waste plans operationalize EPR).
- **Depends on:** Card 2 (coordination unit).

### Card 4 — DRS national rollout
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A,R)
- **Key actors:** PROs (R for operation, ?), GIZ (consulted, ran pilot)
- **Scope:** plastic, glass, aluminum bottles, 0.15-2L
- **Status:** Secondary legislation passed 2024; Jan 2025 launch planned but unverified (issue #4). Legal base re-strengthened in new Law.
- **Depends on:** Card 3 (EPR architecture).

### Card 5 — Performance-based municipal financing
- **Lead:** MMPHI (policy A) + MoFin (operational R, controls transfers)
- **Key actors:** KOM (recipient)
- **Status:** Doesn't exist. MPG-CE 2020-2022 (€7M → +20pp coverage) was the proof-of-concept; CE-PG €20M is the proposed scaled-up successor.
- **Depends on:** Card 1 (data system to measure performance).

### Card 6 — Mandatory source separation (recyclables + bio)
- **Lead:** MMPHI sets the rule
- **Key actors:** KOM (operate), CIT (must separate — *the unrecognized R*), operators (R for separated collection)
- **Status:** Limited; ~5% bio-waste currently separated. Requires infrastructure investment.

### Card 7 — National investment program: recycling, composting, sorting infrastructure
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A) + MoFin (R, allocates)
- **Key actors:** EU (RGF €6B 2024-2027 = largest funding pool), DON (PPP partners)
- **Status:** €77M committed through 2030 in current strategy. CE-PG is one delivery vehicle.

### Card 8 — C&D waste recovery system (80% target)
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A)
- **Key actors:** IND/CON (R, must comply — but most-resistant actor), KOM (enforcement)
- **Status:** Target on paper; non-compliance is documented largest illegal-dumping driver.
- **Hard cell:** the actor most resistant to the rule is the one who has to do it.

### Card 9 — Industrial symbiosis platform
- **Lead:** MINT (A,R) — the only card MINT leads
- **Key actors:** IND (participate), donors (consulted)
- **Status:** Doesn't exist. Trepça lead-battery recycling is the closest existing example (issue #5).

### Card 10 — Landfill tax to incentivize recycling
- **Lead:** MoFin (A,R) — legislative amendment + revenue
- **Status:** Doesn't exist. UK comparison ~£126/ton; Ireland ~€80/ton. INDEP/KAS proposes gradual introduction to avoid spiking illegal dumping.
- **Funds:** Cards 7 (investment) and 14 (innovation finance).

### Card 11 — Reuse + repair centers in main municipalities
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A) + KOM (R, operate)
- **Key actors:** NGO (R, run centers — common civil-society pattern), CIT (R, must use), DON (PPP)
- **Status:** Limited. Tied to incoming Civic Amenity Centers in new law.

### Card 12 — Green Public Procurement requirements
- **Lead:** MoFin (A,R) — Public Procurement Regulatory Commission, under MoFin
- **Key actors:** IND/CON (suppliers, consulted)
- **Status:** Currently weak. Procurement law amendment needed.

### Card 13 — Strengthen enforcement vs illegal dumping
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A) + KOM (operational R)
- **Key actors:** INSP (municipal inspectors, R); CIT (R via reporting — **HackTheTrash is exactly the citizen-side tool this calls for**)
- **Status:** Weak. Inspection capacity underfunded; near-zero fines on construction sites despite documented dumping.

### Card 14 — Financial schemes for CE innovation
- **Lead:** MoFin (A) + DON (R, funding + grants)
- **Key actors:** MINT (consulted, industrial-policy angle)
- **Status:** Doesn't exist as a coordinated fund. Various donor-specific lines.

### Card 15 — National education + awareness program for CE
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A) + MoEd (R, curriculum)
- **Key actors:** CIT (R, audience whose behavior must change), NGO (campaign delivery)
- **Status:** Weak. Circular economy not in school curricula.

## DYVÓ cards (16-21) — plastic + commercial focus

### Card 16 — Administrative Instruction on prevention/reduction of single-use plastics
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A,R) — AI is a ministerial instrument, not parliamentary law
- **Key actors:** PROs (R, compliance once EPR exists)
- **Scope:** Concrete bans on plastic bags, bottles, straws, disposable containers
- **Depends on:** Card 3 (EPR architecture).

### Card 17 — Rigorous taxation system for plastic producers + importers
- **Lead:** MoFin (A,R) — fiscal instrument, more aggressive than landfill tax
- **Key actors:** IND/CON producers (R, must comply), COMM importers (R, must comply), EU (acquis-alignment context)
- **Status:** Doesn't exist. Most aggressive fiscal lever in the deck.
- **Funds:** Card 7 (plastic recycling + sorting infrastructure piece).

### Card 18 — Pay-As-You-Throw (PAYT) for commercial waste
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A) + KOM (R, operate metering)
- **Key actors:** COMM (R, must comply), CIT (potentially extends later)
- **Status:** Doesn't exist. Needs working volumetric measurement, which Pristina/KOM doesn't have.
- **Depends on:** Card 1 (data system) + Card 6 (source separation infrastructure for measurement).

### Card 19 — Business waste responsibility (mandatory waste management plans)
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A) + INSP (R, enforce — load-bearing)
- **Key actors:** IND/CON (R, must draft + comply), COMM (R, must draft + comply)
- **Status:** Builds on EPR principle, operationalizes per-business. Without inspections, plans become shelfware.
- **Depends on:** Card 3 (EPR).

### Card 20 — Source-separation infrastructure with incentive policies
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A) + KOM (R, operate)
- **Key actors:** MoFin (consulted, tax-incentive design), CIT (R, must separate), DON (PPP)
- **Status:** Mirrors Card 6 but adds the carrot (tax reductions, household rewards) to the stick.
- **Plays after:** Card 6.

### Card 21 — Awareness campaigns
- **Lead:** MMPHI (A) + MoEd (R, curriculum)
- **Key actors:** PRESS (R, channel — DYVÓ adds this explicitly), NGO (R, delivery), CIT (R, audience)
- **Plays alongside:** Card 15 — they're two halves of one motion.

## Where the deck concentrates accountability

| Actor | Total A roles | Notable Rs |
|---|---|---|
| **MMPHI** | **12 of 21** (~57%) | Plus R on cards 4, 16 |
| MoFin | **4** (cards 5, 10, 12, 17) | Plus A for card 14 |
| MINT | 1 (card 9) | + R on card 9 |
| MoEd | 0 | R on cards 15, 21 |
| CG | 0 | R on card 2 |
| AMMK | 0 | R on card 1 |
| KOM | 0 | R on cards 6, 11, 13, 18, 20 |
| INSP | 0 | **R on card 19** |
| COMM | 0 | **R on cards 17, 18, 19** |
| PRESS | 0 | R on card 21 |

**Reading:** MMPHI is the load-bearing institution for over half the cards. Its capacity is the binding constraint. **MoFin appears in any card with fiscal teeth** (landfill tax, GPP, plastic tax, CE innovation finance, performance-based municipal funding). **COMM is the new compliance bloc** that emerged once DYVÓ's commercial-focused cards joined the deck. **INSP splitting from KOM** is a Sim refinement: enforcement R is structurally different from operational R.

## DYVÓ ↔ INDEP/KAS cross-mapping

| DYVÓ card | Relation to INDEP/KAS |
|---|---|
| 16 (single-use plastics AI) | **Extends 3** (EPR for packaging) — narrower, product-specific bans within the EPR-for-packaging scope. |
| 17 (plastic producer + importer tax) | **More aggressive than 10** (landfill tax). Landfill tax is downstream (disposal); this is upstream (production + import). Treat as separate fiscal instrument, not duplicate. |
| 18 (PAYT for commercial) | **More specific than 5** (performance-based municipal financing). 5 ties municipal *transfers* to recycling outcomes; 18 ties *commercial fees* to volume generated. Different lever, complementary. |
| 19 (business waste responsibility) | **Operationalizes 3 (EPR) + 8 (CDW recovery).** Per-business waste plans are how EPR + CDW recovery actually get enforced on the ground. |
| 20 (source separation + incentives) | **Mirrors 6** (mandatory source separation). DYVÓ adds the incentive layer (tax reductions, household rewards) that INDEP/KAS treats only as a mandate. |
| 21 (awareness campaigns) | **Mirrors 15** (national education + awareness program). DYVÓ adds explicit media + political-actor channels alongside school curriculum. |

DYVÓ's distinctive contributions: **17** (plastic-specific upstream tax) and **18** (PAYT for commercial). Both are policy levers absent from INDEP/KAS. Adding them strengthens the Sim's plastic + commercial-waste play space.

## Suggested play order

```
Phase 1: Foundational infrastructure
  Card 1 (data system) → Card 2 (Coordination Unit)

Phase 2: Fiscal architecture (parallel)
  Card 5 (performance-based funding) ← needs Card 1
  Card 10 (landfill tax)
  Card 17 (plastic producer/importer tax)

Phase 3: Producer responsibility (parallel)
  Card 3 (EPR for priority streams)
  Card 4 (DRS) ← needs Card 3
  Card 16 (single-use plastics AI) ← sits in EPR architecture

Phase 4: Operational deployment (parallel)
  Card 7 (investment program) ← funded by 10 + 17
  Card 8 (C&D recovery)
  Card 11 (reuse + repair centers)
  Card 13 (enforcement vs illegal dumping)
  Card 19 (business waste plans) ← needs Card 3

Phase 5: Behavior + market shaping (parallel)
  Card 6 (source separation mandate)
  Card 9 (industrial symbiosis)
  Card 12 (Green Public Procurement)
  Card 18 (PAYT commercial) ← needs Cards 1 + 6
  Card 20 (source separation + incentives) ← plays after Card 6

Phase 6: Awareness + innovation (parallel)
  Card 14 (CE innovation finance)
  Card 15 (national awareness program)
  Card 21 (DYVÓ awareness campaigns) ← merges with Card 15
```

The plastic-finance card (17) plays early alongside the landfill tax to establish the dual fiscal architecture. DYVÓ's specific implementation cards (16, 18, 19, 20) play in the same windows as their INDEP/KAS parents.

## Caveats + gaps

- **PROs are written into 5 cards but operationally don't exist yet.** Big paper architecture gap; verification issue #4 ongoing.
- **Cross-municipal coordination** between Pristina and surrounding municipalities (sharing Mirash) isn't a card; could be added.
- **Constitutional Court / Supreme Court** review isn't surfaced as a separate node. Cards needing legislative changes (8, 10, 12, 17) could be challenged.
- **Specific timelines** per card aren't set. INDEP/KAS proposes "implementation by 2030" overall but doesn't sequence them. The dependency analysis above is synthesis, not source claim.
